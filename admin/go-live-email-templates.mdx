Clear, well-timed communication is one of the highest-leverage actions you can take to ensure a smooth Zoom Phone launch. These templates cover the full communication arc—from an initial heads-up weeks before cutover to a follow-up survey and advanced training invitation after launch. Customize each template with your organization's specific dates, links, and contact details before sending.

<Info>
  **Estimated time:** 5–10 minutes to customize each template.
  These templates are for Zoom Phone administrators and IT communications teams managing user communications during deployment.
</Info>

## Email 1: Initial announcement (2–3 weeks before go-live)

<Note>
  Send this email 2–3 weeks before go-live to give users enough notice to prepare without creating anxiety. This is an awareness-only message—no action is required from users yet.
</Note>

```text
Subject: Exciting News: We're Upgrading to Zoom Phone on [Date]

Hi Team,

I'm excited to announce that we're upgrading our phone system to Zoom Phone on
[Day, Month Date]. This modern cloud phone system will improve call quality, add
new features, and integrate seamlessly with the tools you already use.

What's Changing:
- Your phone number and extension stay the same
- You'll make and receive calls through the Zoom app (desktop and mobile)
- New desk phones will be deployed to [departments/locations]
- Enhanced features like voicemail-to-email, call recording, and mobile flexibility

What Stays the Same:
- Your direct phone number: [Example format]
- Your extension: [Example format]
- How customers reach you

What You Need to Do:
- Nothing yet! We'll send detailed instructions next week.
- Make sure you have the latest Zoom app installed.
- Watch for training materials coming soon.

Questions? Contact [IT Team/Help Desk] at [email/extension].

We're here to make this transition as smooth as possible!

[Your IT Team]
```

## Email best practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Timing is everything">
    Space emails appropriately to avoid overwhelming users. The cadence above — weeks 2–3, week 1, day-of, 2 hours after, week 1, week 2, weeks 3–4 — provides steady communication without fatigue.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Keep it scannable">
    Use headers, bullet points, and bold text. Busy users should be able to scan and find what they need in 30 seconds. Long paragraphs get skipped.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Include specific dates and times">
    Replace all bracketed placeholders with actual information before sending. Vague "coming soon" creates uncertainty and generates unnecessary support tickets.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Make support easy to find">
    Include support contact information in every email. Users should never have to search for how to get help, especially during a high-stress cutover window.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Celebrate wins">
    Include positive stats and milestones — "We've made 5,000 calls with a 9.5/10 quality score!" — to build user confidence and reinforce that the migration is going well.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Use a friendly, encouraging tone">
    Change is hard. Acknowledge that, celebrate progress, and maintain an upbeat, supportive tone throughout the entire communication sequence.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Common communication mistakes

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Too much information too soon">
    Break information into digestible chunks over multiple emails rather than sending one massive message. Users stop reading when emails are overwhelming.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Technical jargon">
    Write for non-technical users. Avoid terms like "SIP," "provisioning," or "IVR" without a plain-language explanation. If a user wouldn't know the term, define it or use different wording.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="No clear call-to-action">
    Every email should have 1–2 specific things users need to do. Make those actions obvious, easy, and separate from the surrounding text.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Forgetting mobile users">
    Many users read email on their phones. Test your email formatting on a mobile device before sending to ensure it renders correctly.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="One-way communication">
    Encourage questions and feedback. Make it easy for users to reply or reach support directly from any email in the sequence.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
