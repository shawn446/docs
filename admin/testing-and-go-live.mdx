Before you flip the switch, run through every testing phase in this guide to confirm that your Zoom Phone deployment is ready for production traffic. Thorough pre-launch testing is the single best way to prevent support tickets, user frustration, and emergency rollbacks on go-live day. Plan for 2–4 hours of hands-on testing and one full day for the cutover itself.

<Info>
  **Estimated time:** 2–4 hours for testing, 1 day for cutover.
  This checklist is for Zoom Phone administrators who have completed all configuration steps and are ready to test and launch.
</Info>

Before you begin testing, confirm that all of the following are in place:

## Call flow testing

Work through each call flow category below using real user accounts and actual devices—not just admin accounts.

### Basic call testing

<Steps>
  <Step title="Test internal calls">
    Call between two internal extensions and confirm audio is clear in both directions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test inbound external calls">
    Call from an external phone to a user's direct number and verify the call connects properly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test outbound external calls">
    Place a call from a Zoom Phone user to an external phone number.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify audio quality">
    Confirm audio is clear in both directions on every call type.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test voicemail routing">
    Call an offline user and verify the call routes to voicemail. Leave a voicemail and confirm it is delivered and transcribed correctly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test E911">
    Place a test emergency call (911/999) and verify the correct location appears for the caller.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Emergency calling location errors must be resolved before go-live. If the wrong location displays, update the emergency address for the affected site or user and wait 15 minutes for changes to propagate.
</Warning>

### Multi-device testing

<Steps>
  <Step title="Test from all device types">
    Make calls from the desktop app, mobile app, and desk phone.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test simultaneous ring">
    Receive calls on all devices at the same time to confirm ring-all behavior works.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test call handoff">
    Initiate a call on one device and hand it off to another mid-call.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify contact sync">
    Confirm that contacts sync correctly across all devices.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Auto receptionist testing

<Steps>
  <Step title="Call the main company number">
    Dial your main company number from an external phone to verify the auto receptionist answers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test every IVR menu key">
    Press each key in your IVR menu and confirm it routes to the correct destination.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test timeout behavior">
    Let the auto receptionist time out without pressing any key and verify the fallback routing fires.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test invalid key press">
    Press a key not mapped to any option and confirm the system handles it gracefully.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test closed-hours routing">
    Temporarily change the system time (or wait until after hours) to verify calls route correctly when the business is closed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test multi-level IVR navigation">
    If you have nested menus, navigate between them to confirm sub-menus route correctly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Call queue testing

<Steps>
  <Step title="Test queue entry">
    Call a queue and verify hold music plays while waiting.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test call distribution">
    Confirm calls are distributed to queue members according to your configured routing strategy.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test position announcements">
    If position announcements are enabled, verify callers hear their queue position.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test max wait time overflow">
    Let a call exceed the maximum wait time and confirm overflow routing triggers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test overflow when queue is full">
    Fill the queue to capacity and verify new callers are routed to the overflow destination.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test wrap-up time">
    After an agent handles a call, confirm wrap-up time prevents immediate back-to-back calls.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Recording and monitoring testing

<Steps>
  <Step title="Verify automatic recording">
    Confirm that automatic recording starts on calls configured for recording.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test recording consent prompt">
    If your configuration requires a consent prompt, verify it plays at the start of applicable calls.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check recordings in admin portal">
    Confirm recordings appear in the admin portal with transcription.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify PII redaction">
    If PII redaction is enabled, confirm it applies correctly to transcripts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test call monitoring">
    Exercise each monitoring mode—listen, whisper, barge, and takeover—to confirm all four work.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Network and quality testing

<Steps>
  <Step title="Test on office Wi-Fi">
    Make calls from the office Wi-Fi network and monitor quality.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test on remote and home networks">
    Have remote or home-based users make calls to identify any network-specific issues.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test on mobile data">
    Place calls over a cellular data connection to verify mobile performance.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Monitor the call quality dashboard">
    Open the Zoom admin portal call quality dashboard and watch for anomalies during testing.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test during peak usage times">
    Schedule at least one testing session during typical peak hours to catch capacity issues before they affect real calls.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Go-live day checklist

<Tip>
  Launch on a Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday. Avoid Monday (highest call volume, users just returning) and Friday (reduced staff available for support, and issues discovered late Friday are hard to resolve over the weekend).
</Tip>

### Pre-cutover (morning of go-live)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Run a final test on the main number">
    Call the main company number one last time to confirm everything is working before you begin cutover.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm support team is ready">
    Verify that your support team is in place and briefed on the cutover timeline.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send the go-live reminder">
    Send a "go-live today" reminder to all users with the expected cutover time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Back up current configuration">
    Export or document your current configuration before making any cutover changes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm desk phones are online">
    Check the admin portal to verify all desk phones show as online and registered.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### During the cutover window

<Steps>
  <Step title="Complete number porting cutover">
    If your numbers are being ported, execute the final cutover with your carrier. (Skip if porting is already complete.)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Activate all user accounts">
    Enable any accounts that were held in a pre-activation state.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Notify users the system is live">
    Send the "system is live" notification as soon as cutover completes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test the main number immediately">
    Place a call to the main company number the moment cutover completes to confirm inbound calling works.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the call quality dashboard">
    Begin monitoring the call quality dashboard in real time for any spikes in errors or quality degradation.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Immediate post-cutover (first 2 hours)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm key users can make and receive calls">
    Reach out to stakeholders in each department to verify they can place and receive calls.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify call queues are receiving calls">
    Place test calls into each queue and confirm members receive them.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check the auto receptionist">
    Call the main line from an external number to verify the auto receptionist is answering.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Monitor for unusual call failures">
    Watch the call quality dashboard and call logs for any patterns of failed calls.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Address critical issues immediately">
    Have your rollback plan ready. Escalate any critical issue blocking calls immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update leadership on status">
    Send a brief status update to leadership confirming the system is live and how it's performing.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Testing best practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Test as end users would">
    Use actual user accounts and devices to discover issues users will encounter—not just admin accounts. Admin accounts often have elevated permissions that mask real user-facing problems.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Test edge cases">
    Try to break things intentionally: call during the exact minute business hours change, fill a queue to its maximum capacity, and test the longest supported call duration. Edge cases are where deployment issues most often hide.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Document everything">
    Keep a testing log with timestamps, what was tested, the result, and who ran the test. This record is invaluable when troubleshooting issues discovered after go-live.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Schedule testing during business hours">
    Test when your network carries typical load to surface performance issues before they affect real calls.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Quick wins

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Create a test call script" icon="file-lines">
    Build a standardized script that walks testers through every scenario. A shared script ensures consistent coverage and nothing gets missed.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Plan cutover for low-impact time" icon="clock">
    Schedule go-live for early morning, end of day, or the weekend when call volume is lowest to minimize disruption.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Set up a war room" icon="users">
    Have your admin and support team in one place—physical or virtual—on go-live day so you can quickly coordinate and resolve issues.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Enable soft-launch mode" icon="toggle-on">
    Activate a small pilot group 24–48 hours before full cutover to catch last-minute issues with minimal impact.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Common issues during go-live

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Calls not routing to desk phones">
    Verify the desk phone is online and the user is signed in with the correct credentials. Check the admin portal to confirm the device is registered.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Emergency calling shows wrong location">
    Update the emergency address for the affected site or users. Wait 15 minutes for the change to propagate before testing again.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Auto receptionist menu not responding">
    Confirm IVR keys are mapped to destinations. Verify the greeting audio explains the correct menu options so callers know which key to press.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Users can't access the Phone tab">
    Verify the user accepted their account invitation and has signed in at least once. Check that the phone license is activated on the user's account.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Call quality issues (echo, delay, dropped calls)">
    Run a network quality test. Check that firewall rules allow Zoom Phone traffic. Verify QoS settings are configured correctly on the network.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Voicemail notifications not arriving">
    Check spam and junk folders first. Verify that voicemail-to-email is enabled in the user's settings.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
