---
title: "Set Up Call Recording Policies for Zoom Phone"
description: "Configure automatic call recording, consent prompts, transcription, PII redaction, and data retention to meet compliance requirements."
---

Call recording in Zoom Phone gives you a systematic way to capture conversations for compliance, quality assurance, training, and dispute resolution. This guide walks you through enabling automatic recording, configuring consent prompts that meet one-party and two-party consent laws, setting up transcription with PII redaction, and controlling how recordings are stored and accessed.

## Prerequisites

- Admin access to the Zoom web portal with privileges to edit account settings
- Understanding of recording consent laws in your jurisdiction (one-party vs. two-party consent)
- Audio prompts prepared for recording notifications, if using custom prompts
- A decision on which calls to record: inbound, outbound, internal, external, or some combination

## Understanding call recording types

Zoom Phone supports two recording methods:

- **Automatic recording** — Records calls systematically based on policies you configure. Requires admin setup.
- **Ad-hoc recording** — Users start recording manually during a call. User-initiated, no admin policy required.

This guide covers automatic recording setup. Policies can specify call direction (inbound, outbound, or both), call classification (internal, external, or both), consent requirements, and transcription behavior.

<Note>
Internal calls are classified by how they're dialed, not where they route. A call to someone in your account that forwards to an external number is still considered an internal call.
</Note>

## Enabling automatic call recording

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access recording settings">
    Sign in to the Zoom web portal as an admin. Navigate to **Account Management** > **Account Settings** > **Zoom Phone**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable automatic recording">
    Toggle **Automatic Call Recording** to on. If a verification dialog appears, click **Enable** to confirm.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose recording scope">
    Apply the policy at the appropriate scope. You can enable automatic recording for:

    - Specific users
    - Call queues
    - Shared line groups
    - Sites (if using multiple sites)

    <Note>
    To apply recording settings at a nested level (user, group, site, or call queue), follow the same process within each entity's settings rather than at the account level.
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Configuring recording policies

### Set call types to record

Under the automatic recording policy, configure:

- **Call direction** — Select inbound calls, outbound calls, or both.
- **Call classification** — Select internal calls, external calls, or both.

### Configure inbound call prompts

<Steps>
  <Step title="Play a pre-connect prompt to the caller">
    Enable **Play a prompt only to caller before attempting to connect** to notify the caller before connecting them to the user. Select the default prompt, or click **Edit** to choose a custom audio file from the Asset Library.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Provide consent options before recording starts">
    Enable **Provide consent options before recording starts** to give callers the option to opt out of recording.

    - If enabled, the opt-out applies for the entire call duration, including after transfers.
    - Set the number of prompt repeats.
    - Choose the action after no response: connect with recording enabled, connect without recording, or disconnect.
    - Set the timeout duration in seconds under **After user takes no action for**.

    <Warning>
    Call screening does not work when consent options are enabled. Disable call screening for users where consent prompts are active.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure recording start notifications">
    Enable **Play a prompt to call participants when recording has started** to warn participants that recording is active. Choose the audience — all participants or the recording user only — and select or customize the prompt.

    To require explicit consent via keypress, enable **Press 1 to provide consent**. The prompt plays twice; if the participant does not press 1, their continued participation counts as passive consent. Call logs indicate whether consent was active or passive.

    Enable **Play a prompt for calls where recording has already started** to cover transferred calls and shared line scenarios where recording was already in progress when the participant joined.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Configure outbound call notifications

For outbound calls, configure the following:

- **Play a prompt when recording has started** — Enable notification for outbound calls. Choose the audience (all participants or recording user only) and customize the prompt if needed.
- **Press 1 to provide consent** — Same behavior as inbound: requires a keypress or counts continued participation as passive consent.
- **Play a prompt when recording is stopped** — Notifies participants when recording ends. Select the audience for this notification.

## Additional recording settings

<Steps>
  <Step title="Configure the recording beep tone">
    Enable **Play a recording beep tone** to play a periodic audio cue indicating the call is being recorded. Choose the audience (all participants or recording user only), set the beep interval between 5 seconds and 2 minutes, and adjust the volume.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable transcription">
    Enable **Allow call recording transcription** to automatically transcribe recordings. Users can view transcriptions in the Zoom desktop or mobile app.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable PII redaction">
    Enable **Redact personal and sensitive data** to automatically remove personally identifiable information from transcripts. Select a PII Group to define what data to redact.

    <Warning>
    Redacted data cannot be viewed by anyone, including admins. Verify your PII Group configuration before enabling redaction in production.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure recording controls">
    - Enable **Allow extensions to stop and resume automatic recording** to let users pause and resume recording using in-call controls.
    - Enable **Disconnect call if recording fails** to terminate a call if the recording system fails for more than 5 seconds. This setting does not apply to emergency calls.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Accessing call recordings

<Steps>
  <Step title="View recording logs">
    Navigate to **Phone System Management** > **Recordings** to see all recordings for users and call queues. Filter by date range, user, or extension, or search by caller and callee information.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Play and download recordings">
    Click on any recording to play it. Download recordings for archival or analysis. If transcription is enabled, view the transcript alongside the recording.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Share recordings">
    Share recordings with specific phone users. Control whether users can access their own automatic recordings, and set sharing permissions at the account or user level.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Managing recording storage

Zoom Phone stores recordings in the cloud based on your account storage settings.

Navigate to **Account Management** > **Account Settings** > **Recording** to manage:

- **Storage location** — May be subject to data residency requirements depending on your region.
- **Retention policies** — Configure how long recordings are retained before automatic deletion. Set retention periods that satisfy compliance requirements while minimizing storage costs.

## Compliance use cases

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Two-party consent jurisdictions">
    In states or countries requiring all parties to consent to recording (such as California, Florida, and several EU member states), enable **Provide consent options before recording starts** for inbound calls and **Press 1 to provide consent** for outbound calls. This creates a clear, auditable record of consent in your call logs.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Financial services">
    Enable automatic recording for all regulated activities, configure retention policies per applicable regulations (such as MiFID II or FINRA), and enable PII redaction to protect sensitive financial data in transcripts.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Healthcare">
    Enable transcription with PII redaction to protect PHI in call transcripts. Pair with E2EE settings for calls discussing protected health information. See [Security and Compliance](/admin/security-and-compliance) for HIPAA configuration.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Quality assurance and training">
    Use department-level recording policies to target your support or sales teams specifically. Enable transcription to make recordings searchable, and give supervisors access to recordings via recording sharing permissions.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Best practices

<Tip>
Enable transcription to make recordings searchable and easier to review. Transcriptions dramatically reduce time spent finding specific conversations.
</Tip>

<Tip>
Use groups to apply different recording policies to sales, support, and other departments based on their specific compliance needs.
</Tip>

- **Research your legal requirements** before enabling automatic recording. Two-party consent states require all parties to consent, while one-party consent requires only one party to know about recording.
- **Test recording prompts** thoroughly before rolling out organization-wide. Make test calls to verify prompts play correctly, consent options work as expected, and recording quality meets your needs.
- **Inform employees** about recording policies: when calls are recorded, how recordings are used, and who has access. Include this in employee handbooks and training materials.
- **Set retention periods** that meet compliance requirements but minimize data storage and privacy exposure. Don't keep recordings longer than necessary.

## Common issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Consent prompt not playing for transferred calls">
    Enable **Play a prompt for calls where recording has already started**. This ensures transferred callers hear the recording notification even though recording was already active when they joined.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Recording fails to start automatically">
    Verify the user or extension is included in the automatic recording policy scope. Check that recording hasn't been disabled at a group or user level, which would override the account-level policy.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Transcription not appearing for older recordings">
    Transcription only applies to recordings created after the feature was enabled. Existing recordings are not retroactively transcribed.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Users can't access their own recordings">
    Check recording sharing settings to confirm users have permission to access their automatic recordings. This permission is controlled separately from ad-hoc recording access.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
