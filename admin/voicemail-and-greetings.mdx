The Asset Library is Zoom Phone's centralized hub for all audio content in your organization — voicemail greetings, hold music, IVR prompts, and more. Managing audio centrally means you maintain brand consistency and can update greetings across the entire system without hunting through individual extension settings.

**Estimated time:** 15–20 minutes

**Who this is for:** Zoom Phone administrators managing audio assets for their organization

## Prerequisites

- Admin access to Zoom web portal with privilege to edit account settings
- Audio files prepared (if uploading) in MP3 or WAV format, under 10 MB
- Working microphone (if recording directly in Zoom)

## Understanding the Asset Library

The Asset Library supports three methods for creating audio:

| Method | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Text-to-Speech | Convert text to audio using automated voices |
| Record by Computer | Use your microphone to record directly in the browser |
| Upload | Add existing MP3 or WAV files |

Audio can be set to **Public** (available organization-wide) or **Personal** (private to specific users).

## Create text-to-speech audio

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start a new asset">
    - Sign in to the Zoom web portal as an admin
    - Navigate to **Phone System Management** > **Assets Library**
    - Click **Add**, then click the **Text to Speech** tab
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure and create">
    - **Asset Name:** Enter a descriptive internal name
    - **Category:** Select the type (Voicemail greeting, Music on hold, etc.)
    - **Language:** Choose your language
    - **Voice:** Select a voice style
    - Enter your message text in the message box
    - Click **Play** to preview

    <Tip>
    You can add up to 5 message segments that play in sequence. Click **+ Add message** to create multiple parts.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save">
    - Under **Visibility**, choose **Asset Library (Public)** or **Personal Audios**
    - Click **Add**
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Record audio directly

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start recording">
    - In the Asset Library, click **Add**
    - Click the **Record by Computer** tab
    - Allow Zoom to access your microphone when prompted
  </Step>

  <Step title="Record your audio">
    - Enter **Asset Name**, **Category**, and **Language**
    - Click the red record icon to start
    - Click the stop icon when finished
    - Click **Play** to review, or **Record Again** to redo
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save">
    - Choose your visibility setting
    - Click **Add**
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Upload existing audio files

<Steps>
  <Step title="Upload the file">
    - In the Asset Library, click **Add**
    - Click the **Upload** tab
    - Enter **Asset Name**, **Category**, and **Language**
    - Click **Upload** and select your file (MP3 or WAV, under 10 MB)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save">
    - Choose your visibility setting
    - Click **Add**
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Assign audio to extensions

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to the extension">
    - Go to **Phone System Management** > **Users** (or **Call Queues** or **Auto Receptionists**)
    - Click the extension name
    - Click the **Settings** tab
  </Step>

  <Step title="Assign a voicemail greeting">
    - In the **Call Handling** section, locate **Greeting & Leave voicemail instruction**
    - Click **Edit**
    - Select **Choose from Asset Library**
    - Choose your audio asset
    - Click **Save**
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure hold music">
    - Navigate to **Phone** > **Settings**
    - In the **Others** section, next to **Hold Music**, click **Edit**
    - Select **Choose from Asset Library**
    - Choose your audio
    - Click **Save**
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Manage existing assets

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Edit an asset">
    - Click the ellipsis icon next to the asset name
    - Select **Edit**, make your changes, and click **Save**
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Duplicate, download, or delete an asset">
    - Click the ellipsis icon next to the asset name
    - Select **Duplicate** to copy, **Download** to save locally, or **Delete** to remove

    If you delete an asset that is currently in use, you must choose a replacement audio file before confirming the deletion.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Manage permissions">
    - Click the ellipsis icon > **Manage Editors**
    - Add admins, roles, or groups with Edit or Delete permissions
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Configuration best practices

<Tip>
**Use descriptive asset names.** Name assets clearly — for example, "Main-Voicemail-English" or "HoldMusic-Jazz" — so they're easy to identify and find.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Leverage text-to-speech for quick updates.** For time-sensitive messages like holiday closures or emergencies, text-to-speech allows instant updates without recording equipment.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Test audio quality before deploying widely.** Test assets on desk phones, mobile devices, and computers to ensure clarity across all platforms.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Create holiday greetings in advance.** Record and upload holiday closure messages weeks ahead, then schedule them in call handling settings so you're always prepared.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Build a template library.** Create pre-approved greetings for common scenarios — vacation, meetings, after-hours — that users can quickly apply to their own settings.
</Tip>

## Common issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Browser won't allow microphone access">
    Check your browser's address bar for a microphone icon. Click it and allow access. Chrome often hides this permission in the address bar area.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Uploaded audio file rejected">
    Verify the file is in MP3 or WAV format and is under 10 MB. Convert files using audio editing software if needed.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can't find audio asset in dropdown menu">
    Verify the asset has the correct category assigned. Assets only appear in menus that match their category type.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
