Porting your existing business phone numbers to Zoom Phone preserves your established phone presence and avoids disrupting customers or reprinting marketing materials. The process involves submitting a Letter of Authorization (LOA) to your current carrier, who then releases the numbers to Zoom. For up to 100 numbers you can submit the request yourself through the Zoom admin portal; for larger batches you work directly with the Zoom porting team.

**Estimated time:** 15–20 minutes to submit the request (porting itself takes 4–21+ business days)

**Who this is for:** Account Owners, System Admins, Telecom Managers

## Prerequisites

- Zoom Phone licenses purchased and account set up
- List of phone numbers to port
- Recent invoice or bill from your current carrier (within 45 days)
- Account number with your current carrier (especially for wireless or VoIP accounts)
- PIN or passcode if applicable (wireless carriers require this)
- Authorized person's name matching carrier records exactly
- Service address on file with carrier
- Current service must remain active throughout the porting process

## Before you start: critical requirements

<Warning>
**Do not cancel your current phone service.** If you terminate your contract with your current provider before the port completes, you risk permanently losing your phone numbers. Keep your existing service active until Zoom confirms the port is complete.
</Warning>

**Additional requirements to know before submitting:**

- All information you provide on the LOA must exactly match what is on record with your current carrier.
- If you have broadband service installed on any of the numbers you are porting, you will lose that broadband service when the port completes.
- If you have alarm monitoring services on any porting number, your port request may be rejected until those alarm services are moved to a different line.

## Port up to 100 numbers (self-service)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Gather required information">
    Pull your most recent carrier invoice (dated within the past 45 days) and document the following:

    | Field | Notes |
    |-------|-------|
    | Billing Telephone Number (BTN) | Your company's number as shown on the invoice — not the carrier's number and not a toll-free number |
    | Company name | Exactly as shown on the invoice, not the carrier name |
    | Service address | May differ from your billing address |
    | Account Number | Required for all wireless ports and some VoIP providers |
    | PIN/Password | Required for all wireless ports and some VoIP providers |

    Also identify the authorized person name — it must match carrier records exactly.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Access the porting tool">
    - Log into [admin.zoom.us](https://admin.zoom.us)
    - Navigate to **Number Management** > **Phone Numbers**
    - Click **Port Numbers**

    <Note>
    The self-service portal supports up to 100 numbers per request. For 101 or more numbers, see the project port section below.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter numbers to port">
    - Input the phone numbers you want to port (up to 100)
    - Click **Check Portability** to verify the numbers are eligible
    - If the numbers are portable, click **Next**
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose temporary number assignment">
    - Optionally assign ported numbers to an existing phone number, or add users with a temporary number
    - Using a temporary number lets you configure and test routing in Zoom before the final port completes
    - Specifying an existing phone number does not use additional licenses — the number being swapped out will be removed from your account
    - Click **Next**

    <Tip>
    Use temporary numbers to test your Zoom Phone configuration before the final port. This allows a full rehearsal with zero customer impact.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Complete the Letter of Authorization (LOA)">
    Fill out the online LOA form using your carrier invoice:

    - **Billing Telephone Number (BTN):** From your invoice
    - **Company Name:** Exactly as shown on the invoice
    - **Authorized Person:** Name of the person authorized to request the port
    - **Service Address:** Address on file with the carrier
    - **Account Number:** If required by your carrier type
    - **PIN/Password:** If required by your carrier type
    - **CRD (Customer Requested Date):** Your preferred port date (best effort, not guaranteed). If no date is specified, Zoom requests the earliest possible date.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Submit and monitor your request">
    - Review all information for accuracy — most port rejections result from typos
    - Click **Submit**
    - Zoom sends your request to the previous provider to validate the LOA information
    - If accepted, the Number Portability Administration Center (NPAC) works with Zoom Phone and your previous provider to activate the port
    - Track status at **Number Management** > **Phone Numbers** > **Related Features** > **Port History**
  </Step>

  <Step title="Wait for FOC (Firm Order Confirmation)">
    - Hover over **FOC (Firm Order Confirmation)** in the port history to see your confirmed port date
    - Zoom notifies you when the port date is confirmed
    - Do not cancel your existing service until the port is complete
  </Step>

  <Step title="Prepare for port day">
    - US and Canada porting activates at **11:30 am Eastern Time** (this cannot be changed)
    - Pre-assign ported numbers to users and auto attendants in Zoom before the port date
    - Brief users on expected downtime if any
    - Have your support team available during the port window
  </Step>

  <Step title="Verify port completion">
    - Once approved, the numbers appear in the Zoom web portal for management
    - Test inbound calls to all ported numbers
    - Test outbound calling with the new numbers
    - Once your number transfers to Zoom, your old service on those numbers will no longer function
    - You can now safely cancel your service with the previous carrier
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Port 101+ numbers (assisted project port)

For large-scale migrations, submit a support request to the Zoom porting team rather than using the self-service portal.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download and complete required forms">
    Download and complete all applicable documents:

    - [Local LOA template](https://assets.zoom.us/docs/pdf/Zoom%20LOA%20fillable%20form2.pdf) — required for all ports
    - [Toll-Free LOA](https://assets.zoom.us/docs/number-port-requests/us-canada/TF-LOA-FINAL.pdf) — required if porting toll-free numbers
    - [Number Swap Sheet](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/13dmJCaFpfItqT3FXyYNykwwg5-qHf6kN/copy?usp=sharing&ouid=104112262316235439347&rtpof=true&sd=true) — if you have trial or POC numbers you want to swap out when the port completes

    <Note>
    Numbers swapped in are a like-for-like replacement for numbers swapped out. Main company numbers cannot be swapped out at this time — if you need to replace the main line, note this and the team will upload it as a regular number, which you can then swap once ported.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Sign all documents (wet signature only)">
    - Sign the completed LOA by hand — electronic signatures are not accepted
    - Obtain the authorized signature of the person whose name is on the carrier account
    - Scan all signed documents
  </Step>

  <Step title="Gather supporting documentation">
    - Obtain a recent telephone bill related to your porting numbers (ideally dated within the last 30 days)
    - Ensure the bill clearly shows the account name, service address, and all numbers
    - Optionally obtain a **CSR (Customer Service Record)** from your current provider — this document verifies ownership of all phone numbers and can help resolve disputes
  </Step>

  <Step title="Submit a support request">
    Submit your order through the [Zoom support portal](https://support.zoom.com/hc/en/new-request?id=new_request&sys_id=cb06ab4b8702255089a37408dabb3555&ticket_form_id=360000723312) and complete the form with:

    - **Request type:** Zoom Phone Number Porting
    - **Product:** Your product
    - **Email address:** Your email address (confirm it's accurate)
    - **Type of issue:** New Number Port
    - **Porting Country:** United States or Canada (if porting both, select either)
    - **Porting Number Quantity:** 100+
    - **Description:** Include your Zoom Phone Account Number and any notes relevant to the port (such as target port date or known complexities)
    - **Attachments:** Completed and hand-signed LOA, recent telephone bill (within 30 days), and Number Swap Sheet if applicable
  </Step>

  <Step title="Wait for confirmation">
    - A Zoom team member will review and confirm receipt of your request
    - Zoom Phone and its porting partners will send a request to your previous provider to validate the LOA information
    - If accepted, Zoom and the previous provider work together to activate the pending port
    - Once approved, numbers become available to manage in the Zoom web portal
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Pro tips

<Tip>
**Get every field exactly right.** The number one reason for port rejections is information that doesn't match carrier records. Double-check every field against your invoice before submitting.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Use call forwarding during porting.** Contact your current provider about forwarding calls to your Zoom Phone number before submitting the port request. This allows you to test Zoom without any disruption.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Port in phases.** Port department lines first to test the process, then port your main business line last after everything is verified working.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Pre-assign numbers before port day.** Configure where numbers will ring in Zoom before the port completes. This ensures zero downtime when numbers activate.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Keep detailed records.** Screenshot your LOA, FOC date, and all communications. You'll need these if any issues arise.
</Tip>

<Tip>
**Schedule ports outside business hours when possible.** This minimizes customer impact if there is any brief interruption during the port window.
</Tip>

## Common porting issues and solutions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Address mismatch">
    The address on the order doesn't match the losing provider's records. Most providers require the service or physical address, not the billing address.

    **Solution:** Contact your current provider to confirm the exact service address on file before resubmitting.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Account number missing or invalid">
    The provider requires the account number before approving the port. The account number is usually different from your billing telephone number and is found on your bill. This is most common for mobile numbers.

    **Solution:** Find the account number on your invoice — it is not the same as your phone number.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Incorrect Billing Telephone Number (BTN)">
    When Zoom submits the port request, the provider searches for the customer record using the BTN. If the BTN doesn't match, the provider rejects the request.

    **Solution:** Use the main billing number from your invoice, not any of your direct or DID numbers.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="PIN or passcode required or invalid">
    Some providers require a PIN or passcode to authenticate the port request. This is required by all mobile providers.

    **Solution:** Contact your mobile or VoIP provider to set or retrieve your account PIN before resubmitting.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Service disconnected">
    The phone number or the account holding it is disconnected, inactive, or suspended.

    **Solution:** All phone numbers must be active to port. Contact your service provider to re-establish service before submitting the port request.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Name mismatch">
    The business or residential name on the order doesn't match the provider's customer records.

    **Solution:** Use the exact company name as it appears on your invoice — including capitalization and punctuation.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Pending order, lock, or freeze">
    An active order on the account — such as another port request, a feature change, or an address update — prevents your numbers from porting.

    **Solution:** Contact your provider to resolve or cancel any pending changes before resubmitting the port.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Calls still route to old system after port completes">
    This happens when your previous carrier still has your ported numbers in their network. This is known as "Residual Translations." Losing carriers have 24 hours to remove newly ported numbers from their network.

    **Solution:** Contact your old carrier and request immediate removal of the numbers from their network or switch.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
