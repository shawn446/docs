---
title: "Set Up Auto Receptionists and IVR Menus"
description: "Create auto receptionists, configure IVR menus and menu keys, connect to call queues and users, and control business hours routing for your main number."
---

Auto receptionists answer calls automatically with pre-recorded or text-to-speech greetings, then route callers to the right destination. Use this guide to set up professional call handling for your main company number or departmental lines. Auto receptionists provide 24/7 coverage, reduce receptionist workload, and route callers efficiently—whether you need simple direct routing or a multi-level IVR with language and department selection.

<Note>
  Zoom automatically creates a main auto receptionist for each site. This receptionist is assigned to your main company number and cannot be deleted.
</Note>

## Prerequisites

- Admin access to the Zoom web portal with privilege to edit account settings
- An extension number available for the auto receptionist
- Audio greetings prepared or text for text-to-speech (optional)
- Understanding of your desired call routing flow
- Direct phone numbers assigned (optional)

## How auto receptionists route calls

Auto receptionists support four routing modes:

- **Direct routing:** Route immediately to a destination after playing the greeting
- **Interactive Voice Response (IVR):** Present menu options (press 1 for sales, press 2 for support)
- **Time-based routing:** Different routing for business hours, closed hours, and holidays
- **Multi-level IVR:** Create hierarchical menus for complex organizations

## Create an auto receptionist

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access auto receptionists">
    Sign in to the Zoom web portal as an admin, then navigate to **Phone System Management** > **Auto Receptionists** and click **Add**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure basic information">
    Fill in the following fields:

    - **Site:** Select the site if you use multiple sites.
    - **Name:** Enter a descriptive name (e.g., "Main Company Auto Receptionist").
    - **Specify a template:** Check this box to create a reusable template from this auto receptionist.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the initial setup">
    Click **Save**. The portal redirects you to the auto receptionist settings where you continue configuration.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Configure profile settings

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Profile tab">
    Navigate to your auto receptionist and click the **Profile** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set basic details">
    Update the following:

    - **Display Name:** Click **Rename** to change the name.
    - **Extension Number:** Click **Edit** to change the extension.
    - **Time Zone:** Select the appropriate time zone for business hours.
    - **Department:** Enter the department name for organization.
    - **Cost Center:** Enter the cost center for billing and reporting.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign phone numbers">
    Click **Add** next to Number(s) to assign direct phone numbers. For the main auto receptionist, use the dropdown to select the main company number. Blue text indicates currently assigned direct numbers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure additional settings">
    Set the remaining options:

    - **Audio Prompt Language:** Set the language for system prompts.
    - **Default Transcription Language:** Set the language for voicemail transcription.
    - **PIN Code:** Set the PIN for voicemail access.
    - **Voicemail:** Toggle on to share the voicemail inbox with multiple users.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Set up business hours routing

<Steps>
  <Step title="Define business hours">
    Click **Edit** next to Business Hours and set the days and times when the auto receptionist actively routes calls. Hours can span past 11:30 PM for night operations. Use 10-minute increments by manually entering times (e.g., 11:10 PM).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure the greeting">
    Under **Greeting Prompt**, select Default or click **Audio Library** to use custom audio. The greeting plays before routing the caller. Select **Disable** to skip the greeting and route immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set the routing destination">
    Click **Edit** next to Route to and choose from the following options:

    - **Voicemail:** Route to the auto receptionist's voicemail or another extension's voicemail.
    - **Phone User:** Route to a specific user.
    - **Call Queue:** Send to a call queue.
    - **Shared Line Group:** Route to a shared line.
    - **Auto Receptionist:** Chain to another auto receptionist.
    - **Interactive Voice Response (IVR):** Present menu options to the caller.
    - **External Phone Number:** Route outside the system (requires a direct number assigned to this auto receptionist).
    - **Partner Contact Center:** Route to a third-party contact center integration.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Create an IVR menu

IVR menus let callers choose their destination by pressing keys on their keypad.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enable IVR routing">
    In the Business Hours routing section, click **Edit** next to Route to. Select **Interactive Voice Response (IVR)** from the dropdown and click **OK**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure IVR settings">
    Click **Edit** in the IVR Menu section, then set the **IVR Greeting Audio Prompt**:

    - Click **Default** to use the system greeting.
    - Click **Edit** > **Choose from Asset Library** to use custom audio.

    The IVR greeting plays after the auto receptionist greeting and explains the menu options to the caller.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set timeout options">
    Configure what happens if the caller does not press a key:

    - **Caller Takes No Action After:** Set the number of seconds before timeout (applies when voice navigation is enabled).
    - **Play audio prompt:** Set how many times to repeat the prompt (1–3 times).
    - **And then:** Choose what happens at timeout—disconnect or forward to a destination.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable voice navigation (optional)">
    Toggle **Allow callers to navigate IVR by voice** to let callers speak commands instead of pressing keys. Set the **Voice Input Confidence Score** to Low, Medium, or High. Voice navigation supports English only.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure menu keys">
    For each key (0–9, *, #), click **Edit** to assign a routing destination. A typical setup looks like:

    - Key 1: Sales
    - Key 2: Support
    - Key 3: Billing
    - Key 0: Operator / receptionist
    - Key 9: Dial by name directory
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the configuration">
    Click **Save** to apply the IVR settings.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Build multi-level IVR systems

For complex organizations, create hierarchical IVR menus—for example, language selection leading into department selection.

**Planning approach:**

1. **Diagram your IVR flow** — Map out all levels and branches before building.
2. **Start at the bottom** — Create the lowest-level auto receptionists first (those that don't route to other IVRs).
3. **Work your way up** — Build parent IVR menus that route to child auto receptionists.
4. **Test each level** — Verify routing before connecting to higher levels.

**Example structure:**

```
Main Number → Auto Receptionist (Language Selection)
├── Press 1: English → Auto Receptionist (Department Menu)
│   ├── Press 1: Sales Queue
│   ├── Press 2: Support Queue
│   └── Press 0: Operator
└── Press 2: Spanish → Auto Receptionist (Department Menu)
    ├── Press 1: Sales Queue (Spanish-speaking)
    ├── Press 2: Support Queue (Spanish-speaking)
    └── Press 0: Operator (Spanish-speaking)
```

**Implementation steps:**

1. Create department-level auto receptionists (Sales, Support) with IVR menus.
2. Create language-level auto receptionists that route to the department auto receptionists.
3. Assign the main company number to the top-level (language) auto receptionist.

## Configure closed and holiday hours

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set closed hours routing">
    Click **Edit** next to Closed Hours. Configure a different greeting and routing destination for after-hours calls. A common approach is to play a message and then route to voicemail or an on-call user.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure break hours (optional)">
    Click **Edit** next to Break Hours and set temporary routing during lunch breaks or meetings. This is useful for small businesses with limited coverage.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set holiday hours">
    Click **Manage** next to Holiday Hours. Designate specific dates as holidays, create special greetings informing callers of closures, and route to voicemail or an emergency contact.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Manage the main auto receptionist

**Changing the main company number:**

- Navigate to the main auto receptionist (it displays "Main Auto Receptionist" in its name).
- Click the dropdown next to the main company number.
- Select from assigned numbers or click **Select an unassigned number**.
- The previous main number remains assigned or becomes unassigned based on your selection.

**Assigning additional numbers:**

- The main auto receptionist can have multiple direct numbers.
- All numbers route to the same auto receptionist with the same settings.
- Use IVR to route callers differently based on their menu selection.

## Best practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Keep menus simple">
    Limit IVR menus to 5–6 options maximum. Too many choices confuse callers and increase abandonment. If you need more options, use multi-level IVR.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Always offer an operator option">
    Include Key 0 to reach a live person in every IVR menu. Some callers prefer human interaction, and complex issues may require human judgment.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Test with real callers">
    Before launching, have colleagues outside your tech team test the full call flow. They'll catch confusing wording or routing issues you might miss.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Update greetings regularly">
    Keep holiday messages current. Nothing frustrates callers more than hearing "we're closed for Thanksgiving" in January.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Use professional audio">
    Invest in professional voiceover or high-quality text-to-speech. Poor audio quality damages your brand and reduces caller confidence.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Quick wins

<Tip>
  **Create after-hours routing immediately.** Even if business hours are 24/7 initially, set up closed hours routing now. When you need it (holidays, emergencies), it's already configured.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Enable dial by name directory.** Add a directory option (usually Key 9) so callers who know their contact can dial directly without navigating menus.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Use text-to-speech for rapid updates.** For temporary messages (weather closures, event announcements), use text-to-speech. Update the text instantly without recording new audio.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  **Layer IVR for growth.** Start with simple direct routing. As your organization grows, add IVR menus without changing phone numbers or external documentation.
</Tip>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Callers not hearing IVR menu options">
    Verify the IVR greeting audio is uploaded and not set to "Disable." Check that the IVR Menu section has keys configured with routing destinations.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Key presses not working in IVR">
    Ensure each key has routing configured by clicking Edit next to the key. Keys without routing won't respond to caller input.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Main company number routing to wrong auto receptionist">
    If you use multiple sites, the main company number routes to the main auto receptionist of the main site. Check the Company Info page to verify the main site assignment.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Multi-level IVR routing loops back to first menu">
    Verify child auto receptionists exist and have correct routing. Test each level independently before connecting to the parent IVR.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Holiday greeting playing on wrong dates">
    Check the time zone setting in the auto receptionist profile. Holiday dates are based on the auto receptionist's time zone, not the admin's time zone.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
