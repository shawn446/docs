Zoom Phone voicemail keeps a visual inbox of your missed messages — complete with automatic transcription — accessible from the desktop app, mobile app, web portal, or a desk phone. You can record custom greetings for business hours and closed hours separately, receive email notifications with the audio attached, and set a PIN for desk phone access. Getting your voicemail configured correctly ensures callers always hear a professional greeting and you never miss an important message.

## When voicemail activates

Voicemail receives calls in three situations:

- A call goes unanswered after your full ring time
- You're on another call and your busy setting forwards to voicemail
- A call arrives during closed hours or holiday hours and your settings route it to voicemail

## Checking your voicemail

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Desktop app">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open the Voicemail tab">
        Launch the Zoom desktop app and click **Phone** in the left sidebar.

        Click the **Voicemail** tab at the top.
      </Step>
      <Step title="View your messages">
        Unread messages appear with a badge. Each message shows the caller's name or number, the date, and the duration.

        Transcription text displays automatically below each message.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Play a message">
        Click the **play** button on any message. Audio plays through your speakers. Use the pause, rewind, and fast-forward controls as needed.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Mobile app">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Navigate to Voicemail">
        Open the Zoom mobile app. Tap **Phone** at the bottom, then tap the **Voicemail** tab.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Listen to a message">
        Tap any voicemail to expand it. View the transcription, then tap the **play** button to listen.

        Use the playback controls to toggle speaker on/off, adjust volume, or scrub through the message.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Desk phone">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Access the voicemail center">
        Dial **\*86** on your desk phone, or press the dedicated voicemail button if your phone has one.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Enter your PIN">
        When prompted, dial your voicemail PIN followed by **#**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Navigate your messages">
        Press **1** to play all messages, starting with new ones. Follow the audio prompts to manage messages.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Managing voicemail messages

Once you open a message, you can take several actions:

| Action | How to do it |
|--------|-------------|
| Call back | Click or tap the phone icon next to the message |
| Delete | Click or tap the trash icon — messages cannot be recovered once deleted |
| Download | Desktop: right-click → **Save/Download** · Mobile: tap **•••** → **Download** |
| Share | Click or tap the share icon to forward to another Zoom Phone user |
| Mark as read/unread | Toggle read status to track which messages still need attention |

### Reading transcriptions

Transcription appears automatically below each message, usually within seconds of the voicemail arriving. Review the transcription first to get the gist without listening to the full audio — it's especially useful when you're in a quiet zone. Accuracy varies based on audio quality and accents.

## Recording your voicemail greeting

You set and manage greetings through the Zoom web portal. You can record separate greetings for business hours and closed hours.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open call handling settings">
    Sign in to **zoom.us** and go to **Phone → Settings**.

    Scroll to the **Business Hours** or **Closed Hours** section — whichever greeting you want to record — and click **Edit** next to Call Handling.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Navigate to the greeting option">
    Scroll to the **"When a call is not answered"** section. Find the greeting field and click **Audio Library**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose how to create your greeting">
    Select one of three methods:

    - **Record** — use your microphone to record directly
    - **Upload** — select an audio file from your computer
    - **Text-to-Speech** — type text and have it read aloud by the system
  </Step>
  <Step title="Record your greeting">
    If recording, click **Start Recording** and speak clearly. A good example:

    > "You've reached [Your Name]. I'm unavailable right now. Please leave your name, number, and a brief message, and I'll return your call within 24 hours. For urgent matters, press 0 to reach our main line. Thank you!"

    Click **Stop Recording**, then play it back to review.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and apply">
    Name your greeting — for example, "Standard Greeting" or "Out of Office."

    Click **Save**. Select this greeting in your call handling settings so it plays for callers.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The greeting is added to your Zoom Phone audio library when you save it. You can reuse it across business hours and closed hours settings, or swap it out for a different recording at any time.
</Note>

### Greeting best practices

Keep your greeting professional and concise. State your name and organization, explain you're unavailable, ask callers to leave their name, number, and reason for calling, and mention when they can expect a callback.

Record separate greetings for different situations — business hours, after-hours, vacation, and holidays — and switch between them as your availability changes.

<Tip>
  Record greetings in a quiet space with a good microphone. Background noise significantly degrades audio quality for callers.
</Tip>

## Configuring voicemail settings

### Email notifications

When enabled by your admin, you receive an email when a new voicemail arrives. The email includes:

- A notification that a new voicemail is waiting
- The voicemail transcription in the email body
- An audio file attachment (if your admin enables this)
- A direct link to open the message in the Zoom app

Email notification settings are controlled at the account level by your admin.

### Setting your voicemail PIN

Your PIN is used to access voicemail from a desk phone, for hot desking, and for screen lock on compatible devices.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Phone settings">
    Sign in to **zoom.us** and go to **Phone → Settings**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update your PIN">
    Scroll to **PIN Code**. Click **Edit** or **Show**, enter a new PIN, and click **Save**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Your admin may enforce PIN length and complexity requirements. Do not share your PIN — it protects access to your voicemail from desk phones and shared devices.
</Warning>

### App notification preferences

In the Zoom desktop or mobile app, go to **Settings → Notifications** to configure:

- Enable or disable voicemail pop-up notifications
- Set notification sounds
- Choose how badges display on the app icon

## Common issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can't access voicemail from a desk phone">
    Verify your PIN is set correctly in the web portal. If you don't know your PIN, contact your admin to reset it.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Transcription not appearing">
    Transcription may take a few seconds to process after a voicemail arrives. Refresh the app if it doesn't appear after about a minute.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Not receiving voicemail email notifications">
    Check your email notification settings and spam folder. Verify your admin has enabled email notifications at the account level.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Greeting not playing for callers">
    Confirm you saved the greeting after recording and that you selected it in your call handling settings.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Voicemail appears in email but not in the Zoom app">
    Check that you're signed into Zoom. Voicemails sync to the app when it's running and connected to the internet.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
