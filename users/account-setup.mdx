When your admin assigns you a Zoom Phone license, you'll receive an activation email from Zoom. This page walks you through completing that first-time setup, configuring the essential settings that affect your safety and call experience, and finding any setting you need across the web portal, desktop app, and mobile app.

<Info>
  Estimated time: 10 minutes. You'll need your activation email, your Zoom credentials, and your physical work address on hand.
</Info>

## Prerequisites

- A Zoom Phone license assigned by your admin
- Your activation email from Zoom (for first-time setup)
- Access to the Zoom web portal, desktop app, or mobile app

---

## Initial account activation

When your admin assigns you a Zoom Phone license, you'll receive an email to complete your initial setup.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access setup">
    Click the link at the bottom of the email notification, or sign in to the Zoom web portal at zoom.us and click **Phone** in the left navigation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter required information">
    Fill in the following fields:

    - **Country and area code** — The area code is used when you dial local numbers without a full prefix.
    - **Timezone** — Set the correct timezone. This affects several Zoom Phone features including your business hours, closed hours, holiday hours, and desk phone behavior.
    - **PIN** — Enter a voicemail PIN used to check your voicemail on desk phones. You won't need this PIN to check voicemail using the Zoom desktop app, mobile app, or web portal.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete setup">
    Click **Setup**. Your Zoom Phone account is now active and ready to configure.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Accessing your settings

After activation, you can access and customize your Zoom Phone settings from three locations. The web portal has the most complete set of options.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Web portal">
    The web portal is the most comprehensive settings location and the best place for detailed customization.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Sign in">
        Go to **zoom.us** and sign in with your credentials.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Navigate to phone settings">
        Click **Phone** in the left navigation menu, then click the **Settings** tab at the top.
      </Step>
      <Step title="What's available">
        The web portal gives you access to all configuration options in one place:

        - Emergency addresses
        - Business, closed, and holiday hours
        - Call handling and forwarding rules
        - Voicemail greetings and audio library
        - Delegation and monitoring setup
        - Blocked numbers and call restrictions
        - Desk phone configuration and line keys
        - PIN management
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    <Tip>
      Bookmark zoom.us/phone/settings — you'll visit it frequently. It's the single place where every Zoom Phone setting is available.
    </Tip>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Desktop app">
    The desktop app gives you quick access to the most commonly used settings without opening a browser.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open settings">
        Click the **Settings** (gear icon) in the top-right corner of the Zoom desktop app, then select **Phone** from the left menu.
      </Step>
      <Step title="View your phone numbers">
        - **Direct Number** — Displays any direct numbers assigned to you.
        - **Company Number** — Displays the main company number and your extension.
        - Hover over any number to copy it to your clipboard.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Available settings">
        From the desktop app, you can configure:

        - Local dialing country code and area code
        - Emergency address
        - Business hours (click **Manage** to edit)
        - Call queue preferences
        - Call-me-on device selection
        - Videomail greeting

        Desktop-specific options also include:

        - Click-to-dial preferences
        - Hide calls during meetings
        - Call control actions for desk phones
        - Live transcription settings
        - Default language selection
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Mobile app">
    The mobile app provides quick access to on-the-go settings, including temporary call forwarding.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Access settings">
        Tap your profile picture, then tap **Settings**, then tap **Phone**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="View your contact info">
        From your profile, you can view your extension and direct number. Tap any number to copy it to your clipboard.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Available settings">
        From the mobile app, you can configure:

        - Forward calls (quick temporary forwarding)
        - Hide Zoom Phone calls in iOS device call history
        - Ringtones
        - Voicemail prioritization
        - Default transcription language

        Mobile-specific options also include:

        - **Use CallKit always** — When enabled, your iOS or iPadOS device will always use CallKit for incoming call notifications, integrating Zoom Phone with the native iOS call interface.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Essential first settings to configure

Configure these five settings before you start using Zoom Phone. They affect your safety, your callers' experience, and how calls reach you.

### 1. Emergency address

<Warning>
  Setting your emergency address is required for safety and compliance. This address is displayed to first responders when you dial an emergency number. Do not skip this step.
</Warning>

Your emergency address must reflect your actual physical location. Update it whenever you change work locations.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Web portal">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Navigate to emergency address">
        Go to **Phone** → **Settings**, then find the **Emergency Address** section.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Add or edit your address">
        Click **Add** or **Edit**, enter your physical location, and verify the address accuracy using the address validator suggestions.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Desktop or mobile app">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open phone settings">
        Go to **Settings** → **Phone** → **Emergency Address**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Enter your address">
        Click or tap **Add or update personal location**, enter your address, and save.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Tip>
  When working remotely or traveling, update your emergency address from the mobile app so first responders always have your current location.
</Tip>

### 2. Business hours

Set when you're available to receive calls. Calls outside these hours can be forwarded to voicemail, an external number, or other destinations.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access business hours">
    In the web portal, go to **Phone** → **Settings** → **Business Hours**, then click **Edit**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure your schedule">
    Set hours for each day of the week by choosing start and end times. Hours can span past midnight for night shifts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set closed hours behavior">
    Configure what happens to calls received outside your business hours — for example, forward to voicemail, route to an external number, or play a message.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Configure your business hours before making your number public. This ensures proper call routing from day one.
</Tip>

### 3. Voicemail greeting

Record a professional greeting so callers know they've reached the right person and what to expect.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to greetings">
    In the web portal, go to **Phone** → **Settings** → **Business Hours** → **Edit**, then scroll to "When a call is not answered."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Record or upload your greeting">
    Use the Audio Library to create your greeting. You have three options:

    - Record via microphone directly in the browser
    - Upload an existing audio file
    - Use text-to-speech to generate a greeting from typed text
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and apply">
    Name your greeting, then select it in your call handling settings.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Call yourself from another phone after recording to hear exactly how your greeting sounds to callers. Adjust your tone or wording before going live.
</Tip>

### 4. Caller ID settings

Control what phone number is displayed when you make outbound calls.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Web portal">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Navigate to outbound caller ID">
        Go to **Phone** → **Settings**, then find **Outbound Caller ID**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Select your default number">
        Choose which of your assigned numbers displays by default. You can also enable the option to hide your caller ID entirely.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Desktop app (during a call)">
    Before dialing, click the **Caller ID** dropdown in the dial pad and select which assigned number to display. Use this when you want to call on your main business line rather than your direct extension.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### 5. Notification preferences

Control when and how you're alerted to incoming calls.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Desktop">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open notification settings">
        Go to **Settings** → **Notifications**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Configure alerts">
        - Configure call notification sounds
        - Enable or disable pop-up notifications
        - Set a Do Not Disturb schedule
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Mobile">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open notification settings">
        Go to **Settings** → **Notifications**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Configure alerts">
        - Allow push notifications (required to receive calls when the app is closed)
        - Configure ringtone and vibration behavior
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Understanding setting priorities

Some settings are controlled at different levels of the account hierarchy.

**Account level (admin controlled):**

- Company-wide policies and feature availability
- Call recording permissions
- Settings may show a lock icon if restricted by your admin

**User level (you control):**

- Personal call handling rules
- Voicemail greetings
- Business hours
- Call forwarding preferences

<Note>
  If you see a lock icon or a grayed-out setting, your admin has restricted access at the account level. Contact your IT department to request changes.
</Note>

## Common issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can't access Phone settings in the web portal?">
    Your admin may not have activated your Zoom Phone license yet. Check with IT to confirm the license has been assigned to your account.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Emergency address won't save?">
    Make sure the address is properly formatted with a street number, street name, city, state, and ZIP code. Use the address validator suggestions that appear as you type.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Settings changed but not taking effect?">
    Some settings take a few minutes to propagate. Desk phone settings may require a phone reboot before changes apply.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can't find a specific setting?">
    Some settings are only available in the web portal, not the desktop or mobile apps. Check the web portal first before assuming a setting doesn't exist.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Getting a 'Setting locked by administrator' message?">
    Your admin has restricted this setting at the account level. Request changes through your IT department — you cannot override this yourself.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="PIN not working for voicemail?">
    Verify you're entering the PIN set during initial setup. If you've forgotten it, reset it in **Phone Settings** → **PIN Code** in the web portal.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
