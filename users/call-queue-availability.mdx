---
title: "Manage Your Call Queue Availability in Zoom Phone"
description: "Learn how to opt in and out of call queues, manage availability for specific queues, and follow shift start and end routines as a queue member."
---

If your admin has assigned you to one or more call queues, you control whether you receive calls from those queues using a single toggle — or separate toggles for each queue. This setting is completely independent from your Zoom presence status: you can be "Available" in Zoom while opted out of queue calls, or vice versa. Managing your queue availability correctly ensures calls are covered by the right people at the right times.

<Note>
  You must be assigned to at least one call queue by your administrator before the **Receive Queue Calls** option appears in your settings.
</Note>

## Enabling or disabling queue calls

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Desktop app">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open your profile settings">
        Click your **profile picture** in the top-right corner of the Zoom desktop app.

        Select **Phone** from the left menu.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Scroll to Receive Queue Calls">
        Scroll down until you see the **Receive Queue Calls** section.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Toggle your availability">
        **To enable or disable ALL queues at once:**

        Click the **Receive Queue Calls** master toggle. If you previously disabled specific queues individually, re-enabling the master toggle restores your previous per-queue configuration.

        **To enable or disable SPECIFIC queues:**

        Click the individual toggle beside each queue name. This lets you stay active in some queues while opting out of others — useful if you support multiple teams or skill groups.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Web portal">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Sign in to the portal">
        Go to **zoom.us** and sign in.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Navigate to Phone settings">
        Click **Phone** in the left navigation, then click the **Settings** tab.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Find Receive Queue Calls">
        Scroll to the **Receive Queue Calls** section.

        Toggle call reception on or off for all queues or for specific queues individually.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Shift routines and best practices

Consistent habits around queue availability keep your team's call coverage reliable.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Start of shift">
    - Enable the **Receive Queue Calls** toggle
    - Verify your emergency address is current, especially if you're working remotely (Settings → Phone → Emergency Address)
    - Set your Zoom presence status to **Available**
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="End of shift">
    - Disable the **Receive Queue Calls** toggle
    - Complete any after-call work before signing off
    - Set your presence status to **Away** or sign out of Zoom
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="After-call work">
    When you need time between calls to complete notes or follow-up tasks, temporarily disable **Receive Queue Calls**. Re-enable it when you're ready for the next call.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Working remotely">
    Update your emergency address before starting your shift: **Settings → Phone → Emergency Address**.

    Verify your queue reception status after connecting to a new network — changes to your network can occasionally affect Zoom's connection state.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Tips for managing multiple queues

If you're assigned to more than one queue, you don't have to be all-in or all-out. Use the per-queue toggles to stay active in the queues that match your current role or workload.

<Tip>
  Before disabling queue calls during a high-volume period, let your teammates know so they can adjust coverage. Unexpected drops in queue membership during busy times can increase wait times for callers.
</Tip>

<Tip>
  Use the Zoom mobile app to quickly disable queue calls if you need to step away unexpectedly. You don't need to be at your desk to manage your queue availability.
</Tip>

## Common issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Disabled queue calls but still received a call">
    The call may have been routed before your change took effect. Allow 15–30 seconds for the toggle to propagate. If the issue persists, restart the Zoom app.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Receive Queue Calls option not visible">
    You're not currently assigned to any call queues. Contact your admin or supervisor to request queue assignment.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Queue toggle is grayed out">
    Your admin may have forced you out of the queue, or the queue itself may be temporarily disabled. Contact your supervisor to confirm the queue's status.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
