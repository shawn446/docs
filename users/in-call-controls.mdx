Once you're on a call, Zoom Phone gives you a full set of tools to manage the conversation professionally. You can mute yourself, place the caller on hold, transfer to a colleague with or without a brief introduction, merge into a three-way call, record the conversation, park the call for someone else to pick up, or even flip the call to a different device mid-conversation — all without dropping the line.

<Info>
  Estimated time: 15–20 minutes. Prerequisites: Zoom Phone account activated, at least one test call completed, and basic familiarity with making calls. Recording requires admin permissions if your organization has restricted ad hoc recording.
</Info>

---

## The in-call control bar

When you're on an active call, Zoom displays a control bar with all your call management tools.

**Primary controls (always visible):**

- Mute/Unmute
- Keypad
- Audio Settings
- Add Call
- Hold/Unhold
- Transfer
- Record
- Meet (elevate to a Zoom meeting)

**Secondary controls (More menu):**

- Call Park
- Invite to Meeting
- Share Call Details
- Minimize

## Mute and unmute

Control your microphone to eliminate background noise and keep the other party focused on what you're saying.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Mute your microphone">
    Click the **Mute** button in the control bar. Your microphone icon shows a red slash when muted. The other party cannot hear you.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Unmute to speak">
    Click **Unmute**. The microphone icon returns to normal and you can resume speaking.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Keyboard shortcut:** Press and hold the **Spacebar** on desktop to temporarily unmute. Release the spacebar to mute again. This is useful during long calls when you need to interject briefly without clicking.

<Tip>
  Stay muted when you're not speaking to eliminate background noise, especially in open offices or home environments. Adopting "mute by default" as your standard is one of the most professional habits you can build.
</Tip>

## Using the keypad during calls (DTMF tones)

Use the in-call dial pad to navigate automated phone menus and enter information while your call is active.

**When to use the keypad:**

- Navigating phone trees and automated menus ("Press 1 for Sales")
- Entering extension numbers when transferred by an automated system
- Providing account numbers or confirmation codes
- Accessing voicemail systems via DTMF prompts

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the keypad">
    Click **Keypad** in the control bar. The on-screen number pad appears over the call window.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter numbers">
    Click numbers on screen or type on your keyboard. DTMF tones are sent to the other party and numbers appear briefly in the call window.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Close the keypad">
    Click the **X** or anywhere outside the keypad to close it and return to the standard in-call view.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Audio settings during calls

Switch microphones or speakers mid-call without dropping the connection — useful if your headset disconnects or you want to move to speakerphone.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access audio settings">
    Click the **Audio** button in the control bar. A settings panel appears.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Change devices">
    - **Speaker** — Switch your output device (headset, computer speakers, etc.)
    - **Microphone** — Switch your input device
    - Adjust volume sliders for both as needed
  </Step>
  <Step title="Apply changes">
    Audio switches immediately without interrupting the call. Test by speaking briefly to verify the new microphone is working.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  If your audio suddenly stops working mid-call, click **Audio** and verify the correct devices are still selected. Device changes (like plugging in a headset) can sometimes cause Zoom to switch automatically.
</Tip>

## Hold and unhold

Place a caller on hold when you need to step away, look up information, or consult a colleague before responding.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Place the call on hold">
    Click the **Hold** button in the control bar. The caller hears hold music (customizable in the web portal under **Phone** → **Settings** → **Hold Music**). Your line is muted to the caller.
  </Step>
  <Step title="What you can do while the caller is on hold">
    - Look up information in other applications
    - Consult with colleagues
    - Use call flip to switch the call to a different device
    - The call timer continues running
  </Step>
  <Step title="Resume the call">
    Click **Unhold**. The caller is reconnected immediately and you can continue your conversation.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Tell the caller "I'm placing you on hold" before clicking the button. Never assume they can infer what's happening. Also avoid leaving callers on hold for more than 60–90 seconds without checking back in.
</Tip>

## Transferring calls

Route an active call to a colleague, department, or voicemail.

### Transfer types

**Blind transfer (cold transfer):**
- Transfers the call immediately without speaking to the recipient first
- You disconnect as soon as the transfer is initiated
- The fastest method — use when the destination is obvious and no context is needed

**Warm transfer (consultative transfer):**
- Connects you to the recipient before completing the transfer
- Lets you brief them on the caller's needs
- You can complete or cancel the transfer based on their availability

### How to transfer a call

<Steps>
  <Step title="Initiate the transfer">
    Click **Transfer** in the control bar. Your current caller is placed on hold automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the recipient">
    - **Number pad** — Dial a phone number or extension directly
    - **Search contacts** — Type a name to search the internal directory
    
    Click the contact or complete entering the number.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose your transfer method">
    Click the blue **Transfer** button and select your transfer type:

    - **Blind Transfer** — The call transfers immediately. You are disconnected.
    - **Warm Transfer** — You are connected to the recipient first. The original caller remains on hold.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Completing a warm transfer

<Steps>
  <Step title="Speak with the recipient">
    After selecting warm transfer, you're connected to the recipient while the original caller remains on hold hearing music. Brief the recipient about who is calling and why.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete or cancel">
    - Click **Complete Transfer** to connect the caller and recipient, then disconnect yourself.
    - Click **Cancel Transfer** to return to the original caller if the recipient is unavailable or not ready.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Use warm transfers for important calls so you can brief your colleague before they pick up. The 30 seconds of context saves minutes of confusion on the other end.
</Tip>

### Transferring directly to voicemail

<Steps>
  <Step title="Initiate the transfer">
    Click **Transfer** in the control bar. Your current caller is placed on hold automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the voicemail option">
    Choose to transfer directly to someone's voicemail. The caller goes straight to a voicemail greeting and can leave a message without ringing the recipient's devices.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Adding calls and conference calling

Create three-way calls by adding a second participant to your active call.

### Adding a second call

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Add Call">
    Your current caller is placed on hold and the dial pad appears.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Dial the additional party">
    Enter a phone number or search contacts, then click the phone icon to dial.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Manage two separate calls">
    Two separate lines appear in your call window. Click a name or number to switch between callers. Only one caller is active at a time — the other remains on hold.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Merging calls (three-way calling)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click the merge icon">
    After adding a second call, the merge icon appears in your call window. Select which call to merge with the active call.
  </Step>
  <Step title="All parties connect">
    Everyone can hear each other. Standard three-way call functionality applies — if one participant hangs up, the other two continue.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Recording behaves differently when merging calls. The initial recording stops when you merge. You can start a new recording for the merged call.
</Note>

## Recording calls

Document important conversations for training, compliance, or follow-up reference.

### Starting a recording

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Record">
    Click the **Record** button in the control bar. An audio prompt may notify both parties that recording has started (this behavior is configured by your admin). A red recording indicator appears.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Recording runs">
    A timer shows recording duration. All audio is captured and the recording continues until you stop it or the call ends.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Stop recording">
    Click **Stop Recording**. The recording is saved automatically. Access your recordings in **Phone** → **Recordings** tab.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Recording with DTMF codes

If your admin has enabled DTMF recording codes, you can also control recording from a desk phone:

- Press **#1** to start recording
- Press **#2** to stop recording

### Important recording notes

**How recording interacts with other call actions:**

- **Transfers** — Recording stops when you transfer. With warm transfers, you can resume recording before completing the transfer.
- **Adding calls** — Recording continues for the initial call. A new recording starts for the added call.
- **Merging calls** — Recording stops when you merge calls. Start a new recording for the merged call.
- **Elevating to meeting** — Recording stops when you convert the call to a Zoom meeting.

**Admin controls that may affect you:**

- Your admin may disable ad hoc recording entirely
- Automatic recording may be enabled by policy, starting at call connection
- You may be able to pause and resume automatic recordings
- Admins can access call queue recordings

<Warning>
  Verify that recording is legal in your jurisdiction before recording calls. Most jurisdictions require you to notify callers that you're recording. Follow your company's recording consent and retention policies.
</Warning>

## Elevating calls to Zoom meetings

Convert a phone call into a full Zoom meeting with video and screen sharing.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Meet">
    Click the **Meet** button during an active call. The call converts to an instant Zoom meeting.
  </Step>
  <Step title="What happens">
    - Any active phone call recording stops
    - The caller joins as a phone participant in the meeting
    - You can now share your screen, enable video, and use all meeting features
    - Additional participants can join via the meeting link
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use cases">
    This is most useful when you need to:

    - Share your screen during a support call
    - Add video to a discussion that started as a voice call
    - Add more than three participants to the conversation
    - Use meeting recording instead of call recording
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Additional controls (More menu)

### Call park

Hold a call so a colleague can retrieve it from any device.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Park the call">
    Click **More** in the control bar and select **Park** from the menu.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Note the park code">
    The system assigns a park code (usually 3–4 digits). Share this code with the colleague who will pick up the call.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Colleague retrieves the call">
    They dial the park code from their phone. The call transfers to them immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

A common use case: the front desk parks a call for someone who isn't at their desk, then pages them the park code.

### Invite to meeting

Send the caller an invitation to a scheduled Zoom meeting.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the invite option">
    Click **More** in the control bar and select **Invite to Meeting**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter meeting details">
    Enter the Meeting ID or select from your calendar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Invitation sent">
    The caller receives the meeting details and can join when they're ready.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Share call details

Send call information to a phone number via SMS.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the share option">
    Click **More** in the control bar and select **Share Call Details**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the recipient number">
    Enter the phone number you want to send the details to.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Details sent">
    An SMS with caller information is sent to the recipient. Useful for logging calls or passing information to a colleague for follow-up.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Minimize

Click **More** → **Minimize** to minimize the call window while keeping the call active. The call continues in the background with a small call indicator remaining visible. Click the indicator to restore full controls.

## Call quality indicators

Monitor your call quality in real-time from the control bar.

Three icons in the control bar show:

- **Encryption icon** — Confirms your call is secured end-to-end
- **Quality indicators** — Signal strength and connection quality
- **Network icon** — Current network status

If you experience poor quality:

1. Click the icons to view detailed statistics
2. Check latency, jitter, and packet loss values
3. Troubleshoot your network connection or switch to a different device or network

## Common issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Record button is grayed out">
    Your admin has disabled ad hoc recording, enabled automatic recording that prevents manual control, or your account doesn't have recording permissions. Contact your admin to request access.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Caller can't hear me after I unmute">
    Click **Audio** and verify the correct microphone is selected. Check that the volume slider isn't set to zero.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Warm transfer failed">
    The recipient may be on Do Not Disturb or have all their devices offline. Try a blind transfer to their voicemail instead, or call them on a different number to check availability first.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Hold music isn't playing for callers">
    Hold music is configured in the web portal under **Phone** → **Settings** → **Hold Music**. Contact your admin if you need it customized or if it's not playing at all.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Recording didn't capture the whole call">
    Recordings stop during transfers and when merging calls. Check your recordings tab — you may have multiple segments from a single call session.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
