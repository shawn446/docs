title: "Record Calls and Access Your Recordings in Zoom Phone"
description: "Start and stop ad-hoc call recordings, understand consent announcements, find and download recordings from history, and follow compliance best practices."

Zoom Phone call recording lets you capture phone conversations for documentation, training, quality assurance, and compliance. Your admin controls whether recording is available to you and whether it starts automatically or requires you to trigger it manually. If ad-hoc recording is enabled, a **Record** button appears in your call controls. Recordings are saved to your call history within minutes and include a transcription if your admin has enabled that feature.

<Warning>
  Always comply with local laws regarding call recording consent. Many jurisdictions require all parties to consent before a call is recorded. Familiarize yourself with the rules in your region before recording.
</Warning>

## Recording types

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Ad-hoc recording">
    You manually start and stop recording during a call. The **Record** button appears in your call controls when your admin has enabled this permission. You choose which calls to record.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Automatic recording">
    Calls record automatically based on admin-configured rules — for example, all inbound calls, all outbound calls, or all queue calls. Depending on your admin settings, you may be able to stop or pause an automatic recording during a call.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## How participants are notified

Your admin configures what callers and agents hear when recording begins:

- **Audio prompt** — "This call may be recorded for quality and training purposes." Plays when recording starts to all participants present at that moment.
- **Explicit consent prompt** — Callers hear the prompt twice and must press 1 to consent. If no response is given, passive consent is assumed. Call logs indicate whether consent was active or passive.
- **Periodic beep tone** — A beep plays at configurable intervals (every 5 seconds to every 2 minutes) to remind all parties the call is being recorded. The volume and audience (all participants or recording user only) are configured by your admin.

## Starting and stopping a recording (ad-hoc)

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Desktop app">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="During an active call, find the Record button">
        Look at the call controls at the bottom of the screen. The **Record** button (red circle icon) appears if your admin has granted you recording permission.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Start recording">
        Click **Record**. An audio prompt plays to both parties if configured by your admin. A recording indicator appears on screen.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Stop recording">
        Click **Stop Recording**. The recording saves automatically. Both parties may hear a notification that recording has stopped.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Mobile app">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Access additional controls">
        During an active call, tap **More** (three dots) to open additional call controls.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Start recording">
        Select **Record** from the menu. A notification plays to both parties. A recording icon displays on screen.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Stop recording">
        Tap **More** again and select **Stop Recording**. The file saves automatically.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="DTMF keypad">
    You can also control recording using keypad codes — useful on desk phones and mobile devices:

    - Press **\*1** to start recording
    - Press **\*1** again to stop recording
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Managing automatic recordings

If your admin enabled automatic recording, calls begin recording as soon as they connect.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Identify the active recording">
    When a call connects, check for the recording indicator on screen. Automatic recording starts without any action from you.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Stop the recording (if permitted)">
    Click or tap **Stop Recording**. The recording pauses but is not deleted.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Resume recording (optional)">
    Click or tap **Record** to continue. Recording resumes in the same file, with timestamps marking the stop and resume points.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Not all admins allow you to stop automatic recordings. If the stop button doesn't appear, your admin has disabled that option.
</Note>

## Accessing your recordings

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Desktop app">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open your call history">
        Click **Phone** in the left sidebar. Click the **History** tab.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Find recorded calls">
        Recorded calls show a recording icon. Recordings appear after processing — usually within a few minutes of the call ending.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Play a recording">
        Click on the call entry, then click the **play** button to listen. View the transcription below the player if enabled.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Download the recording">
        Right-click the recording entry and select **Download** or **Save**. The audio file saves to your computer.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Mobile app">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Navigate to history">
        Tap the **Phone** tab, then tap **History**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Find the recorded call">
        Look for the recording icon on call entries. Tap the call to expand it.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Play or share">
        Tap the **play** button to listen. Use the share icon to send the recording to others if your admin has permitted sharing.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Web portal">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Sign in and open history">
        Go to **zoom.us** and click **Phone → History**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="View recordings">
        Filter by recorded calls. Each entry shows the recording duration, date, and transcription if available.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Download or share">
        Click the **ellipsis (•••)** next to the recording and select your preferred action — download or share.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Recording transcription

If your admin has enabled transcription, Zoom automatically transcribes recordings after each call completes.

**Transcription features:**
- Text appears below the recording in your history
- Transcriptions are searchable — use keywords to jump to specific parts of a conversation
- Timestamps mark each segment
- Speaker identification is included when available

**Redacted transcriptions:** Your admin may enable automatic redaction of personal data from transcripts. Zoom analyzes the recording and replaces sensitive information — such as credit card numbers, social security numbers, and other PII — with generic placeholder text. Redacted data cannot be viewed by anyone once it is removed.

## Recording on shared lines (delegation)

Call recordings on shared lines belong exclusively to the person who started the recording:

- If the delegator records a call, only the delegator can access that recording
- If the delegate records a call, only the delegate can access that recording
- Both parties see the call history entry, but neither can see the other's recordings

This design protects privacy and ensures proper record ownership.

## Best practices

**Announce recording at the start of each call.** Even when automated prompts play, stating "For quality assurance, this call is being recorded — do you consent?" is a courteous and often legally required practice.

**Know your local recording laws.** Research one-party versus two-party consent requirements in your region and in the caller's region. Some US states and many countries require all parties to consent.

**Avoid recording sensitive information unnecessarily.** If permitted, pause recording when callers provide passwords, payment details, or other confidential information that doesn't need to be retained.

**Review recordings promptly.** Listen while the conversation is fresh to capture action items, follow-up commitments, and key details before you move on.

**Use transcription search to save time.** Scan transcription text to find specific topics rather than listening to an entire recording from start to finish.

**Verify recording is active at the start of long calls.** Confirm the recording indicator appears when the call begins. Discovering a failure after a 45-minute conversation is much harder to remedy than catching it in the first 30 seconds.

## Common issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Record button doesn't appear during a call">
    Your admin has not enabled ad-hoc recording for your account. Contact your IT team to request recording permissions.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Recording stopped automatically">
    Check whether your admin has set time limits or storage restrictions. Automatic stop behavior is configured at the admin level.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can't find a recording in history">
    Recordings take a few minutes to process after a call ends. Refresh your history after 5 minutes. Confirm that recording actually started during the call by checking for the recording indicator.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Recording has no audio">
    Check your microphone settings. If the recording file is silent, your microphone may not have been active during the call.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Transcription not appearing">
    Transcription is an admin-enabled feature and may take longer to process than the audio. Check back after 10–15 minutes.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Caller hung up when the recording prompt played">
    Some callers decline to consent to recording. Be prepared to continue without recording or contact them in advance to let them know calls may be recorded.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
