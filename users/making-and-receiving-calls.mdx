---
title: "Make and Receive Calls with Zoom Phone"
description: "Place outbound calls via dial pad, contacts, or history, answer and decline inbound calls, and manage simultaneous calls across all your devices."
---

Zoom Phone gives you multiple ways to place and receive calls, so you can work however fits your workflow best. You can dial from the number pad, click a contact, copy-paste a number from an email, or call back directly from your history — and on the receiving end, you have full control over how you answer, decline, or manage calls when you're already on the line.

<Info>
  Estimated time: 10–15 minutes. Prerequisites: Zoom Phone account activated, Zoom desktop client or mobile app installed and signed in, audio devices configured (headset, speakers, microphone), and valid phone numbers or contacts to call.
</Info>

---

## Making your first call

### Using the dial pad (desktop)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access the Phone tab">
    Sign in to the Zoom desktop client and click **Phone** in the left navigation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Dial the number">
    Use the on-screen number pad or type directly on your keyboard. Enter the full phone number including area code. For international calls, include the country code (for example, +44 for the UK).

    Click the blue phone icon to place the call.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select caller ID (optional)">
    Before dialing, click the **Caller ID** dropdown to choose which of your assigned numbers displays to the recipient. This is useful if you have multiple direct numbers or extensions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Zoom Phone automatically converts vanity numbers (like 1-800-FLOWERS) to digits when you dial. Just type the letters and Zoom handles the conversion.
</Tip>

### Calling from contacts

The fastest way to call colleagues and frequently-dialed numbers.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Search contacts">
    In the Phone tab, start typing a name or number in the search box above the dial pad. Zoom searches your internal directory, external contacts, and synced contacts simultaneously. Click the search result to call.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Handle multiple numbers">
    If a contact has multiple phone numbers, click the right arrow icon to view all available numbers with their labels (Mobile, Office, Home). Select the specific number you want to call.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use starred contacts">
    Click the **Starred** tab above the dial pad to see your favorite contacts. Hover over a contact and click the phone icon to call instantly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Calling synced contacts

If you've synced Google, Office 365, or Exchange contacts, you can access them through the Contacts directory.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to contacts">
    Click **Contacts** in the Zoom desktop client navigation. In the **My Groups** section, click **Cloud Contacts**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select and call">
    Browse or search for the contact. Click **Call** or hover over their name and click the phone icon. If they have multiple numbers, select which one to dial.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Synced contacts are only available in the desktop app — not on mobile or desk phones.
</Note>

### Calling from call history

Quick redial for numbers you've recently called or received calls from.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open call history">
    In the Phone tab, click the **History** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Dial from history">
    Double-click any entry to instantly redial. You can also hover over an entry and click the phone icon.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Copy-paste a number

The quickest method when you have a number in an email or document.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Copy the number">
    Highlight and copy the phone number from any application.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Paste and dial">
    Click in the dial pad text field, paste the number with Ctrl+V (or Cmd+V on Mac), and click the phone icon to call.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Using click-to-dial

Call numbers directly from other applications without copying and pasting.

When click-to-dial is enabled, phone numbers in web browsers, Outlook, and other apps become clickable. Hover over any phone number in any application — the number becomes highlighted and clickable. Click it to launch a dial in Zoom Phone, confirm, and the call connects.

<Note>
  Admins may need to enable click-to-dial. Check your Phone settings in the web portal if the feature isn't working.
</Note>

### Calling internal extensions

Internal calls to colleagues are fast — you don't need the full phone number.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Dial the extension">
    In the dial pad, enter just the extension number (for example, 1234). No area code or full number required for internal calls.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Or search the internal directory">
    Type a colleague's name in the search box. Internal contacts appear at the top of results. Click to call their extension directly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="View extensions on hover">
    Hover over names in your Contacts directory to see their extension number alongside their name.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Making calls from mobile

The Zoom mobile app provides the same calling features, optimized for your smartphone.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Zoom mobile app">
    Launch Zoom on iOS or Android and tap **Phone** in the bottom navigation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Place your call">
    Tap the dial pad icon, enter the number or search contacts, and tap the green call button.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Access contacts">
    Tap **Contacts** to view your directory. Search or browse for the person, tap their name, then tap the phone icon.
  </Step>
</Steps>

When receiving calls on mobile:

- A lock screen notification appears with the caller's name or number
- Accept or decline directly from the lock screen
- The same options are available as on desktop: Accept, Decline, Decline with Text
- If CallKit is enabled on iOS, calls integrate with the native iOS call interface

## Receiving calls

When someone calls your Zoom Phone number, you'll see a notification with caller information and several response options.

### Call notification behavior

**When you're not on a call:**
- A notification appears with the caller's name or number
- Your ringtone plays on all configured devices
- You have several response options

**When you're already on a call:**
- A notification appears without interrupting your current call
- Different options let you manage both calls
- You can choose to hold, end, or ignore the incoming call

### Answering options

<Steps>
  <Step title="Accept">
    Click **Accept** to answer the call. It connects immediately on your current device, and your in-call status syncs to desk phones.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Decline">
    Click **Decline** to refuse the call. The caller is routed to voicemail (or disconnected, depending on your settings). Check your settings under "When not answered, calls will be" to control this behavior.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Decline with Text (mobile)">
    On mobile, select a pre-written text message to send to the caller. The message is sent while the caller is routed to voicemail. Useful for letting callers know you'll call back shortly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Close (×)">
    Click the **X** to dismiss the notification on your desktop. The call continues ringing on your other devices. Use this when you want to answer on your desk phone instead.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Silence">
    Stops the ringtone on all devices without sending the caller to voicemail. The call continues to ring silently, giving you time to decide whether to answer.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Handling calls when you're already on a call

If a second call comes in while you're already talking, you'll see these options:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Hold & Accept">
    Places your current call on hold and answers the incoming call. You can then switch between the two calls or merge them into a three-way call using the in-call controls.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send to Voicemail">
    Continues your current call and routes the incoming call directly to voicemail — no interruption to your active conversation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="End & Accept">
    Ends your current call and immediately answers the incoming call. Use this when you need to prioritize the new caller.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Understanding call status

Your Zoom status affects how calls reach you.

### Do Not Disturb mode

**How to enable:**

- Click your profile picture in the top right
- Select **Do Not Disturb**
- Choose a duration (20 minutes to 24 hours)

**What happens when Do Not Disturb is active:**

- Incoming calls don't ring on any device
- All calls route directly to voicemail
- Your status shows that you're unavailable
- Perfect for focused work or back-to-back meetings

**Ending Do Not Disturb:**

- It disables automatically after the selected duration
- To end it early, click your profile picture and turn it off manually
- Calls resume ringing normally after it ends

### Other status indicators

- **Available (green)** — Ready to receive calls
- **In a meeting** — Calls may be silenced based on your settings
- **Away** — Set automatically after a period of inactivity

## Calling best practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Test before important calls">
    Make a test call to a colleague to verify your audio setup before client calls. It's better to catch microphone or speaker issues with a friendly voice than in front of a customer.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Use Starred contacts for VIPs">
    Star your most frequent contacts for one-click calling. This saves time and reduces dialing errors for the numbers you call most.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Set up Do Not Disturb for focus time">
    Block out 2–3 hours of deep work by enabling Do Not Disturb. Calls go to voicemail and you check them on your schedule.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Answer on the right device">
    If you want to answer on your desk phone, click the X to dismiss the desktop notification. The call keeps ringing on your desk phone.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Change caller ID for external calls">
    When calling customers or vendors, select your main business line as the caller ID instead of your personal extension. This presents a consistent identity to external contacts.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Quick wins

- **Keyboard number pad dialing** — Start typing numbers on your keyboard when in the Phone tab. No need to click the dial pad — it's significantly faster.
- **Double-click to redial** — In your call history, double-click any number to instantly redial. No searching or typing needed.
- **Ctrl+F to find contacts** — When in the Phone tab, press Ctrl+F (Cmd+F on Mac) to quickly search contacts without clicking the search box.
- **Custom ringtones** — Assign different ringtones to your extension vs. direct number so you know which line is ringing without looking at your screen.

## Common issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Call notification didn't appear">
    Check if you're in Do Not Disturb mode. Also verify that desktop notifications are enabled in your operating system notification settings.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="I can't hear the caller">
    Check your audio device selection in **Settings** → **Audio**. Make sure the correct speaker is selected and the volume is turned up.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Contact search isn't finding people">
    Internal contacts should appear automatically. For external contacts, verify you've synced Google or Office 365 contacts in your settings.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Caller ID shows the wrong number">
    Change your default outbound caller ID in the web portal under **Phone** → **Settings** → **Outbound Caller ID**, or select it from the dropdown before each call.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Number pad isn't responding">
    Click inside the dial pad text field first, or just start typing on your keyboard. The number pad accepts both input methods.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
