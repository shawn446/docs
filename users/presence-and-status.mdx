Your presence status in Zoom tells colleagues whether you're available, occupied, or not to be disturbed — and it directly affects how call notifications reach you. You can set your status manually at any time from the desktop app, mobile app, or web, and Zoom also updates it automatically when you join meetings or go idle. Taking a minute to manage your status keeps your team informed and helps you protect uninterrupted work time.

## Status options and what they mean

| Status | Icon | How it's set | What it signals |
|--------|------|--------------|-----------------|
| Available | 🟢 (desktop) / 📱 (mobile) | Manual or automatic when active | Signed in and ready for calls and chats |
| Busy | 🔴 | Manual — clears after 24 hours | Working but may not respond immediately |
| Away | 🕖 | Automatic after inactivity, or manual | Inactive or stepped away |
| Do Not Disturb | ⛔ | Manual | Blocking all interruptions |
| In a Meeting | 🎥 | Automatic | Currently in a Zoom meeting |
| On a Call | 📞 | Automatic | Currently on a Zoom Phone call |

## Setting your status

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Desktop app">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open the status menu">
        Click your profile picture in the top-right corner of the Zoom desktop app.

        Your current status appears below your name.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Choose your status">
        Click your current status to open the menu. Select from:

        - Available
        - Busy
        - Away
        - Do Not Disturb
      </Step>
      <Step title="Confirm the change">
        Your new status displays immediately. The icon next to your name updates across Zoom for all your contacts.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Mobile app">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open status settings">
        Tap **More** in the bottom navigation bar.

        Tap your profile details at the top of the screen.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Change your status">
        Tap your current status, then select the desired status from the list.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Confirm">
        Your status updates immediately and is visible to all your contacts.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Web app">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Click your profile picture">
        In the top-right corner of **zoom.us**, click your profile picture.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Select a status">
        Choose from the available status options. Your status updates across all platforms.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## How your status affects incoming calls

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Available">
    Your phone rings normally on all devices. Call notifications appear as usual. No impact on incoming calls.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Busy">
    Calls still ring through normally. Busy indicates to colleagues that you might not answer, but it doesn't block calls. It clears automatically after 24 hours if you forget to change it back.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Away">
    Calls ring normally. Away sets automatically after a period of inactivity, or you can set it manually before stepping away from your desk.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do Not Disturb">
    When set to Do Not Disturb, you won't receive pop-up notifications for chat or Zoom Phone calls in the desktop or mobile app. Calls may still go to voicemail depending on your call handling settings. Use DND for deep focus time or after work hours.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="In a Meeting / On a Call (automatic)">
    Zoom sets these automatically when you join a meeting or answer a phone call. Your status reverts to its previous value when the meeting or call ends. Colleagues see you're occupied, and calls may trigger call waiting based on your settings.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Note>
  Do Not Disturb blocks pop-up notifications but may not block all calls depending on your call forwarding rules. Review your call handling settings if you want calls to go directly to voicemail when DND is active.
</Note>

## Adding a custom status message

A status message gives colleagues more context — for example, "In client meetings until 3pm" or "Working from home today."

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Desktop">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open your profile">
        Click your profile picture in the top-right corner.

        Click **Set Status Message**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Enter your message">
        Type your custom message. Choose how long to display it:

        - 1 hour
        - 4 hours
        - Today
        - This week
        - Custom date and time
        - Never (until you clear it manually)
      </Step>
      <Step title="Save">
        Click **Save**. Your message displays immediately to all chat users who can view your profile.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Mobile">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Access status message settings">
        Tap **More** → your profile.

        Tap **Set Status Message** (Android) or **Status Message** (iOS).
      </Step>
      <Step title="Create your message">
        Enter your status message text. Set an expiration time using the **Clear After** menu.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Save">
        Tap **Save** in the top-right corner. Your message displays to all contacts.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Automatic status changes

Zoom updates your status automatically in several situations:

- **Inactivity** — After a period of inactivity (typically around 5 minutes), your status may change to Away. When you return to your computer, it changes back to Available. Configure the inactivity timeout in **Settings → General**.
- **During meetings** — Status changes to "In a Meeting" when you join a Zoom meeting. It reverts to your previous status when you leave.
- **During calls** — Status shows "On a Call" during Zoom Phone calls and returns to your previous status when the call ends.

## Scheduling Do Not Disturb

Set recurring DND hours so it activates automatically — for example, every weekday evening to maintain work-life boundaries.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open notification settings">
    - **Desktop:** Click **Settings** (gear icon) → **Notifications**
    - **Mobile:** Tap **More** → **Settings** → **Notifications**
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure the DND schedule">
    Toggle on **Schedule Do Not Disturb**. Set your start and end times, then choose which days of the week the schedule applies.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Do Not Disturb activates automatically during your scheduled times and returns to Available outside those hours.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Use Busy status during focused work blocks when you still want urgent calls to ring through. Save Do Not Disturb for times when you want no call or chat interruptions at all.
</Tip>

## Common issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Status not updating automatically">
    Check your inactivity settings in **Settings → General**. Ensure the Zoom app isn't running only in the background.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do Not Disturb not blocking calls">
    DND blocks pop-up notifications but may not stop calls from ringing depending on your call handling configuration. Review your call forwarding rules in Phone Settings.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Status keeps changing to Away unexpectedly">
    Increase your inactivity timeout or disable automatic Away status in **Settings → General**.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Status message not visible to others">
    Confirm you clicked **Save** after creating the message. Status messages sync across devices within a few seconds.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can't set a Do Not Disturb schedule">
    This feature may be disabled by your admin. Contact your IT team to request access.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
