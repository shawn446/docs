The Zoom Phone mobile app lets you make and receive business calls from anywhere using your company phone number — not your personal cellular number. Whether you work remotely, travel frequently, or just need to stay reachable outside the office, the mobile app gives you the same professional calling experience you get on the desktop, including hold, transfer, park, and call recording.

<Info>
  Estimated time: 15–20 minutes. Prerequisites: Zoom Phone license activated; iOS device running iOS 9 or higher, or Android device running Android 5.0 or higher; stable Wi-Fi or cellular data connection; access to the App Store or Google Play Store.
</Info>

---

## Installing the Zoom mobile app

Zoom Phone functionality is built into the standard Zoom Workplace app — there's no separate phone app to download.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="iOS (iPhone/iPad)">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open the App Store">
        Tap the **App Store** icon on your device.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Search for Zoom">
        Tap **Search** at the bottom right, type "Zoom" or "Zoom Workplace" in the search box, and select **ZOOM Cloud Meetings** or **Zoom Workplace** from the results.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Install the app">
        Tap **Get** or the download icon. Authenticate with Face ID, Touch ID, or your Apple ID password, then wait for installation to complete.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Launch Zoom">
        Tap **Open** or find the Zoom icon on your home screen. The app is now ready for sign-in and setup.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Android">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open Google Play Store">
        Tap the **Play Store** icon on your device.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Search for Zoom">
        Tap the search bar at the top, type "Zoom" or "Zoom Workplace," and select **Zoom Workplace** or **Zoom Cloud Meetings** from the results.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Install the app">
        Tap **Install**, accept permissions when prompted, and wait for the download and installation to complete.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Launch Zoom">
        Tap **Open** or find Zoom in your app drawer. The app is ready to sign in and configure.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Signing in and initial setup

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign in to Zoom">
    Open the Zoom app and tap **Sign In**. Enter your work email and password, or tap **SSO** if your organization uses single sign-on.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify phone access">
    After signing in, look for the **Phone** icon in the bottom navigation bar. If you don't see it, sign out and sign back in. If it's still missing, contact your admin to confirm your Zoom Phone license is active.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable notifications (critical)">
    When prompted, tap **Allow** for notifications. This ensures you receive incoming calls even when the app is closed. You can also enable notifications later in your device **Settings** → **Zoom**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure location services">
    Enable location permissions for emergency calling (E911):

    - **iOS:** Settings → Zoom → Location → While Using the App
    - **Android:** Settings → Apps → Zoom → Permissions → Location → Allow
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Without notification permissions, you will not receive incoming calls when the app is closed. Enable this permission during initial setup — it's required for the mobile app to function as a business phone.
</Warning>

## Navigating the mobile phone interface

Tap **Phone** in the bottom navigation bar to access all calling features.

**Phone tab sections:**

- **Dial pad** — Make calls by entering numbers directly
- **Contacts** — Access your directory (Starred, Internal, External)
- **History** — View call logs and missed calls
- **Voicemail** — Listen to messages and read transcriptions
- **SMS** — Send and receive text messages from your business number
- **Lines** — View shared lines (if applicable)

**At the top of the screen:**

- Search bar for quick contact or number lookup
- Caller ID selector (if you have multiple numbers assigned)

## Making calls from mobile

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Dial pad">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open the Phone tab">
        Tap **Phone** in the bottom navigation. The dial pad appears automatically.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Enter the number">
        Tap numbers on screen or use your device's keyboard. Include the area code for external calls. For international calls, add the country code (+ then the country code).
      </Step>
      <Step title="Select caller ID (if needed)">
        Tap the dropdown at the top to choose which of your assigned numbers displays to the recipient.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Place the call">
        Tap the green phone icon. The call connects.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Contacts">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Access contacts">
        In the Phone tab, tap **Contacts** or use the search bar. Browse **Starred**, **Internal**, or **External** contact sections.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Find your contact">
        Scroll or search by name. Tap the contact's name to open their profile.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Initiate the call">
        Tap the phone icon next to their name. If they have multiple numbers, select which one to call.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Call history">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open History tab">
        In the Phone tab, tap **History**. View all recent calls: incoming, outgoing, and missed.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Tap to call">
        Tap the phone icon next to any entry. The call connects to that number immediately.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Receiving calls on mobile

Zoom Phone calls reach you even when the app is closed in the background.

**When the app is open:**
- A full-screen call notification appears with the caller's name or number
- All response options are immediately accessible

**When the app is closed:**
- A lock screen notification appears
- Your ringtone plays
- Accept or decline directly from the lock screen
- On iOS with CallKit enabled, the call looks and behaves like a native phone call

### Answering options

<Steps>
  <Step title="Accept">
    Tap **Accept** or swipe to answer. The call connects immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Decline">
    Tap **Decline**. The caller is sent to voicemail based on your call handling settings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Decline with Message (SMS)">
    Tap **Decline with Message** and select a pre-written text response. The message is sent to the caller while they're routed to voicemail. Note: this only works for callers on mobile phones, not desk phones.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ignore">
    Tap **Ignore** (Android) or dismiss the notification. This stops ringing on your device but the call continues ringing on your other devices. Useful when you're on a shared line and someone else can answer.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  If you have CallKit enabled on iOS, Zoom Phone calls integrate with your phone's native call interface and appear in your iPhone's recent calls list. You can answer without unlocking and use Bluetooth controls.
</Tip>

## Mobile in-call controls

All the same controls available on the desktop are accessible on mobile, with a touch-optimized interface.

**Primary controls:**

- **Mute/Unmute** — Tap the microphone icon
- **Keypad** — Access the dial pad for navigating phone menus and entering DTMF tones
- **Audio** — Switch between speaker, Bluetooth, or earpiece
- **Add Call** — Conference in another person
- **Hold** — Place the caller on hold
- **Transfer** — Transfer to another number or extension
- **Meet** — Elevate the call to a Zoom meeting
- **More** — Access additional options

**Additional controls (More menu):**

- Park call
- Invite to meeting
- Share call details
- Minimize call window

## Managing voicemail on mobile

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Voicemail tab">
    In the Phone tab, tap **Voicemail**. You'll see a list of all messages with caller information and timestamps.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Listen to messages">
    Tap the play button on any message. A voicemail transcription appears automatically while the message plays. Tap the speaker icon to toggle speakerphone.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Take action on messages">
    - **Delete** — Tap the trash icon
    - **Call back** — Tap the phone icon to return the call immediately
    - **Forward** — Tap the forward icon to share the voicemail via text or email
    - **Mark as read/unread** — Swipe or long-press the message
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Voicemail notifications:**
- Push notification when a new voicemail arrives
- Email notification with a transcription attached
- Badge count on the Phone tab
- Transcriptions are available within seconds of the message being left

<Tip>
  Swipe left on a voicemail message for a quick delete without tapping into it. Swipe right to mark it as read or unread.
</Tip>

## Using SMS on mobile

Send and receive text messages from your business number, not your personal cell number.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the SMS tab">
    In the Phone tab, tap **SMS**. View all your existing conversations.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start a new message">
    Tap the pencil or compose icon. Enter the recipient's phone number at the top.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Type and send">
    Type your message in the text field and tap send. The message sends from your business phone number.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Unread messages show a badge count on the SMS tab. Tap any conversation to view the full thread. Messages sync across all your devices — desktop, mobile, and web.

## Call forwarding on mobile

Temporarily redirect calls while you're away or in a meeting.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access call forwarding">
    Tap your profile picture (top left or right), then tap **Forward Calls**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a forwarding destination">
    - **Name or number** — Forward to a specific colleague or external number
    - **Voicemail/Videomail** — Send all calls directly to voicemail
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set a time limit">
    Choose a duration: 30 minutes, 1 hour, 2 hours, and so on. Or select **Until I turn it off** for an indefinite forward.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enable forwarding">
    Tap to activate. A confirmation banner appears at the top of the screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Disable when ready">
    Tap **Turn Off** in the persistent reminder banner. Calls resume ringing normally on your devices.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  "Play my voicemail greeting" is enabled by default when forwarding. If the forwarded person doesn't answer, the call returns to your voicemail — not theirs.
</Note>

## Switching between Wi-Fi and cellular

### Using Wi-Fi (recommended)

Zoom Phone uses Wi-Fi by default. Wi-Fi provides the best call quality, uses no cellular minutes, and gives you access to all Zoom Phone features. Use Wi-Fi whenever possible.

### Switching to cellular mid-call

If your Wi-Fi becomes unstable during an active call:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access the switch option">
    During the call, look for the **Switch to Cellular** option and confirm your mobile phone number.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tap Switch">
    The call transitions to your cellular network using your carrier's voice minutes. The call continues without dropping.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Understand the limitations">
    When on cellular, you lose Zoom Phone features including transfer, hold, park, recording, and conferencing. The call is treated as a regular cellular call. Switch back to Wi-Fi when possible to restore full features.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Adjusting mobile settings

### Audio and ringtone settings

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access settings">
    Tap **More** → **Settings** → **Meetings**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure audio">
    - **Ringtone volume** — Adjust the slider
    - **Ringtones** — Set different tones for video calls, extension calls, and direct number calls
    - **Audio profile** — Choose a noise suppression level
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Notification settings

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="In-app settings">
    Go to **More** → **Settings** → **Meetings** → **General** to enable or disable specific notification types.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Device-level settings">
    - **iOS:** Device Settings → Zoom → Notifications
    - **Android:** Settings → Apps → Zoom → Notifications

    Control sound, vibration, and lock screen notification appearance from here.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### CallKit integration (iOS only)

CallKit makes Zoom Phone feel like a native part of iOS.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enable CallKit">
    Go to **More** → **Settings** → **Meetings** → **Use CallKit to receive calls** and toggle it on.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Benefits of CallKit">
    - Answer calls directly from the lock screen like a normal phone call
    - Zoom calls appear in iPhone's native recent calls list
    - Integrates with car Bluetooth systems
    - Enables Siri voice commands for calls
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Mobile app best practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Enable all permissions immediately">
    Grant notifications, microphone, contacts, and location permissions during setup. Missing any of these creates frustrating gaps — like not receiving calls, not hearing callers, or not having access to your contact directory.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Keep the app updated">
    Enable auto-updates in your app store settings. New features and bug fixes arrive frequently for Zoom Phone, and running an outdated version is a common cause of unexpected issues.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Use Wi-Fi for quality calls">
    Whenever possible, connect to Wi-Fi before placing or receiving important calls. Cellular data works but Wi-Fi provides better audio quality and gives you access to all in-call features.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Enable CallKit on iOS">
    This makes Zoom Phone feel native to your iPhone. You can answer calls without unlocking your screen, see calls in your recent calls history, and use your car's Bluetooth controls seamlessly.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Set up call forwarding before meetings">
    Before important meetings, forward your calls to voicemail for the duration. This prevents your phone from ringing mid-meeting and ensures callers aren't left hanging without any response.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Quick wins

- **Use voice search** — Tap the microphone in the search bar and say the person's name. Faster than typing on a small keyboard.
- **Add to Starred from history** — Long-press any number in your call history to add it to Starred contacts for future quick access.
- **Background App Refresh (iOS)** — Enable in Settings → General → Background App Refresh → Zoom to ensure you receive calls reliably when the app is in the background.

## Common issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="I'm not receiving incoming calls">
    Verify notifications are enabled in both Zoom app settings and your device settings. Sign out and back in. On iOS, ensure Background App Refresh is turned on for Zoom.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="The Phone tab isn't showing in the app">
    Your Zoom Phone license may not be activated. Contact your admin to confirm. Also try signing out completely and signing back in — this forces a license sync.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Call quality is poor on cellular">
    Switch to Wi-Fi when possible. On cellular, ensure you have strong signal (3 or more bars). Close other apps that may be using data in the background.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Voicemail transcriptions aren't appearing">
    Transcriptions take 30–60 seconds to generate after a voicemail is left. If they're still missing after a few minutes, contact your admin to verify that transcription is enabled for your account.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Battery drains quickly when using Zoom">
    Zoom can use significant battery when constantly connected over cellular. Enable battery optimization in your device settings, or switch to Wi-Fi to reduce power consumption.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can't switch to cellular during a call">
    This feature requires your mobile carrier number to be linked to your Zoom Phone account. Contact your admin to configure this connection.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
