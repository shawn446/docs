---
title: "Monitor and Coach Calls as a Supervisor in Zoom Phone"
description: "Use listen, whisper, barge, and takeover modes to monitor team calls in real time, coach agents during live calls, and intervene when escalation is needed."
---

Call monitoring in Zoom Phone lets supervisors, managers, and quality assurance personnel join active calls without disrupting the conversation — or with full participation when the situation demands it. You can listen silently, whisper coaching advice that only the agent hears, join the call openly, or take full control of the call if needed. Each mode serves a different purpose, and you can switch between most of them during a single monitoring session.

<Note>
  Call monitoring is not enabled by default. Your admin must enable monitoring and add you to a monitoring group before you can use these features. Contact your Zoom Phone administrator to request access.
</Note>

## Who can monitor and who can be monitored

**Users who can monitor:**
- Supervisors and managers (for their assigned team members)
- Call queue administrators (for queue calls)
- Shared line group members (for each other)
- Executives and assistants in delegation setups

**Users who can be monitored:**
- Phone users assigned to monitoring groups
- Call queue members during queue calls
- Common area phones
- Shared line group members

## Monitoring modes

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Listen (silent monitoring)" icon="ear">
    You hear both sides of the conversation. Neither party hears you. Use this for quality assurance, performance assessment, and identifying coaching opportunities.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Whisper (coach mode)" icon="comment">
    You hear both sides. Only the agent hears you — the caller does not. Use this to provide real-time guidance during active calls without the caller knowing.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Barge (join the call)" icon="phone-volume">
    You join as a full participant. All three parties hear each other. Use this for escalations, complex issues, or when a customer requests to speak with a manager.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Takeover (transfer control)" icon="right-left">
    You take complete control of the call. The original agent is removed. Use this for serious escalations, VIP customers, or crisis situations that require full ownership.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Warning>
  Once you use **Takeover**, you cannot switch back to listen, whisper, or barge mode. The agent is fully removed from the call. Use takeover only when full ownership is necessary.
</Warning>

## Starting a monitoring session

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Desktop app">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open the Lines tab">
        Click **Phone** in the left sidebar. Click the **Lines** tab.
      </Step>
      <Step title="View monitored users">
        In the **MONITORING** section, you'll see your assigned team members along with their current call status — available, busy, or on a call — and call duration for active calls.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Start monitoring">
        When a team member is on a call, click their name. Choose your monitoring mode:

        - Click **Listen** (ear icon) for silent monitoring
        - Click **Whisper** (microphone icon) for coaching mode
        - Click **More** → **Barge** (conference icon) to join the call
        - Click **More** → **Take Over** (transfer icon) to take control
      </Step>
      <Step title="Switch modes during monitoring">
        While listening, you can click **Barge** to join the conversation or **Takeover** to take full control. You can also switch between listen and whisper modes using the in-call controls.

        Note: once you take over a call, you cannot switch back to any other monitoring mode.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Desk phone">
    After your admin sets up call monitoring, a BLF (Busy Lamp Field) line key is automatically added to your desk phone for each user you can monitor.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="View line key status">
        Look at your desk phone's home screen. Monitored users appear in the line key list. A red icon or solid red light indicates an active call.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Start monitoring">
        Press and hold the line key for the monitored user. Your phone automatically connects you in listen mode.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Use monitoring controls">
        Follow the on-screen prompts or function keys to switch modes. Specific key assignments vary by desk phone model.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Monitoring notifications

**For the agent being monitored:** Depending on your admin's settings, the agent may hear a disclaimer prompt when monitoring begins. Some organizations notify agents for transparency; others use silent monitoring. Your admin determines which approach applies to your team.

**For the caller:**
- In listen and whisper modes, callers typically hear nothing
- When you barge, they may hear an entry tone (if enabled by admin)
- At the start of the call, callers may hear a prompt such as "This call may be monitored for quality assurance purposes"

## Monitoring different call types

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Call queue monitoring">

  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Shared line monitoring">
    Members of a shared line group can monitor each other. This supports peer coaching and collaborative team development.
  </Accordion>

## Best practices for supervisors

**Set clear expectations with your team.** Inform team members that calls may be monitored for training and quality improvement. Explain the purpose and establish a regular monitoring schedule so it feels fair.

**Use listen before deciding to intervene.** Assess the full situation before whispering or barging. If the agent is handling the call well, silent monitoring may be all you need.

**Use whisper sparingly and briefly.** Constant coaching during a call distracts agents. Wait for the right moment and keep your guidance short and actionable. Whisper positive reinforcement too — "Great job handling that objection" builds confidence.

**Barge thoughtfully.** When joining a call, introduce yourself professionally: "Hi, this is [Name] — I'm joining to assist." Support your agent rather than undermining them in front of the customer.

**Reserve takeover for genuine escalations.** Use it for VIP customers, crisis situations, or when an agent is truly struggling. Debrief with the agent after the call so they understand what happened and why.

**Document your observations.** Take notes during monitoring sessions, capturing both strengths and areas for improvement. Use your notes in structured coaching conversations after the call — not just in-the-moment corrections.

**Create monitoring scorecards.** Standardize what you evaluate so feedback is consistent across your team. Rotate your focus from week to week — openings one week, objection handling the next — so agents improve specific skills over time.

## Common issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can't see monitored users in the Lines tab">
    Your admin hasn't added you to a monitoring group yet. Request access from your Zoom Phone administrator.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Monitoring option is grayed out">
    The user may not be on an active call, or they may be on a call that cannot be monitored (such as a personal call or an external conference).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="No audio when listening to a call">
    Check your device audio settings. Verify your speaker volume is up and the correct output device is selected.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Monitored person can hear me during listen mode">
    Your microphone may be active. Confirm you're in listen mode and not accidentally in whisper or barge mode, then mute your microphone.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Desk phone monitoring keys not appearing">
    Your desk phone may need to restart after the admin adds you to a monitoring group. Power cycle the phone and check again.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can't switch back to whisper after takeover">
    This is expected behavior. Takeover is a one-way transition — you cannot return to any other monitoring mode once you take over a call.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
