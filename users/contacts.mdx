title: "Manage Contacts in Zoom Phone"
description: "Add, organize, and call contacts in Zoom Phone — including manual entry, call history saves, and syncing from your company directory, Google, or Outlook."
---

Zoom Phone gives you access to several contact sources in one place: your company's internal directory, personal contacts you add or import, and optionally synced contacts from Google or Outlook. Keeping your contacts organized means faster dialing and less time hunting for numbers. You can add contacts manually from any device, star the ones you call most, and build them up from your call history as you go.

## Contact types in Zoom Phone

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Internal contacts" icon="building">
    Users on the same Zoom account appear automatically. No setup is needed — your company directory is always available.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Personal contacts" icon="address-book">
    Contacts you add or import manually. Store names, phone numbers, email addresses, and company names.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Synced contacts" icon="rotate">
    If your admin enables it, contacts from Google or Outlook sync automatically to Zoom Phone.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Starred contacts" icon="star">
    Contacts you've marked as favorites. They appear in a dedicated Starred section for quick access across the Phone, Team Chat, and Contacts tabs.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Adding contacts manually

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Desktop app">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open the Contacts tab">
        Launch the Zoom desktop app and click **Contacts** in the left sidebar.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Add a new contact">
        Click the **+** icon or **Add Contact** button.

        Enter the contact's information:
        - Name (required)
        - Phone number
        - Email address
        - Company name
      </Step>
      <Step title="Save">
        Click **Save**. The contact appears in your personal contacts list.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Mobile app">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Navigate to Contacts">
        Open the Zoom mobile app and tap **Contacts** at the bottom of the screen.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Add a contact">
        Tap the **+** icon in the top-right corner.

        Enter the contact details and tap **Save**.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Web portal">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Access personal contacts">
        Sign in to **zoom.us** and click **Personal Contacts** in the navigation menu.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Add a contact">
        Click **Add Contact**. Enter the contact's name and details.

        Phone numbers must be in E.164 format (for example, +12025550123).
      </Step>
      <Step title="Save">
        Click **Save**.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Note>
  When entering phone numbers manually in the web portal, use E.164 format: a plus sign, followed by the country code, followed by the full number with no spaces or dashes.
</Note>

## Adding contacts from call history

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your call history">
    Go to the **Phone** tab and click **History**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the caller">
    Locate the caller you want to save in your history list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add to contacts">
    Click or tap the caller's number, then select **Add to Contacts**. Enter any additional details and save.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Organizing your contacts

### Starring important contacts

Starring a contact adds them to the Starred section so you can reach them instantly.

**Desktop:** Search for or find the contact, then click the **star icon** next to their name. The contact appears in the Starred section under the Phone, Team Chat, and Contacts tabs.

**Mobile:** Tap the contact's name, then tap the **star icon** in the top-right corner.

<Tip>
  Star your top 10–15 most-called contacts. Too many starred contacts reduces the benefit — the Starred section works best as a short list of your most frequent calls.
</Tip>

### Editing a contact

**Desktop:** Find the contact in the Contacts tab, hover over their name, click the **ellipsis (•••)** icon, and select **Edit Contact**. Make your changes and click **Save**.

**Web portal:** Go to Personal Contacts, click **Edit** to the right of the contact, make your changes, and click **Save**.

### Deleting a contact

<Warning>
  Deleting a contact removes them from your personal contacts list. Unless you block them, they can still request to be a contact again.
</Warning>

**Desktop:** Hover over the contact name, click the **ellipsis (•••)** icon, select **Delete Contact**, and confirm.

**Mobile:** Tap the contact, tap the **ellipsis (•••)** in the top-right corner, tap **Delete Contact**, and confirm.

## Calling from your contacts

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find the contact">
    Search by name or browse your contacts list. Check the Starred section for frequently called contacts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Initiate the call">
    - **Desktop:** Click the phone icon next to the contact's name.
    - **Mobile:** Tap the contact, then tap the phone icon.

    The call connects through Zoom Phone.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Alternative method">
    Right-click (desktop) or long-press (mobile) any contact and select **Call** from the menu.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Contact not appearing after adding">
    Refresh the app or switch tabs. Check that the contact was saved successfully by viewing it in the web portal.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can't call a contact even though they're saved">
    Verify that the phone number format is correct. Try editing the contact and re-entering the number in E.164 format with the country code.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Duplicate contacts showing up">
    This happens when the same person exists in both your personal contacts and a synced source. Delete the duplicate from your personal contacts list.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Contact information not syncing across devices">
    Ensure you're signed into the same Zoom account on all devices and that you have an active internet connection.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Tip>
  Use descriptive names for external contacts — for example, "John Smith - ABC Company" instead of just "John Smith" — to distinguish between contacts with the same name.
</Tip>
